# Fire Crystal Upgrades (FC1→FC10)

::: info Quick take
Reaching FC10 on your Furnace alone costs **11,610 Fire Crystals and 870 Refined Fire Crystals**, spread across ten tiers that each contain four sub-levels. Refine early, prioritize the Furnace, and treat Refined Fire Crystals as your real bottleneck from FC6 on.
:::

## What the Fire Crystal Era Is

At Furnace 30 the game switches to a new upgrade track. Instead of leveling 30→31, your Furnace advances through Fire Crystal tiers: **FC1 through FC10**. Each major tier is broken into four sub-levels you clear in sequence — FC1-1, FC1-2, FC1-3, FC1-4, then FC2, and so on.

Two things stay true from the Furnace era. Your Furnace still gates every other building — no camp, Embassy, or other structure can exceed your Furnace's current FC level. And you still upgrade prerequisite buildings along the way. What changes is the currency: Fire Crystal upgrades cost **Fire Crystals (FC)**, and from the FC6 tier onward they also cost **Refined Fire Crystals (RFC)**.

Fire Crystals come from Intel Missions, Arena and event shops, Castle Battle, and the Fire Crystal event chests. Refined Fire Crystals are made by refining raw Fire Crystals in the Crystal Laboratory — see [Refined Fire Crystals](#refined-fire-crystals-the-real-bottleneck) below.

## Refined Fire Crystals: The Real Bottleneck

Raw Fire Crystals are easy to stockpile from events and shops. Refined Fire Crystals are not — you can only make them yourself, on a daily clock, and missed days are gone for good.

- **Source:** The Crystal Laboratory. You spend raw Fire Crystals to refine them into RFC.
- **Daily cap:** The Lab refines a limited number per day, and the cap rises with Lab level (around 7 per day at Lab Level 6).
- **When it unlocks:** The Crystal Laboratory opens partway through a state's life; RFC production typically begins well after the Lab itself appears. Timing varies by state.

Because RFC is capped per day and required in large amounts at FC9–FC10 (180 then 420 on the Furnace alone), it — not raw FC, not resources — becomes the gate on how fast you finish the FC era.

::: tip
Start refining the day the Lab allows it, even if you're nowhere near FC6. RFC banked early is free progress later. An idle refining queue is the FC-era equivalent of an idle construction queue — unrecoverable lost capacity.
:::

## When It Unlocks (Approximate)

Fire Crystal tiers open in waves as a state ages. These are rough server-day windows and vary by state:

| Window           | Unlocks            |
| ---------------- | ------------------ |
| Around Day 60    | FC1–FC3            |
| Around Day 150   | FC4–FC5            |
| Around Day 315   | FC6–FC8 (RFC era)  |
| Around Day 500   | FC9–FC10           |

If your state is close to opening a higher bracket, it can be worth holding a finished upgrade until the new tier is live so your speedups land on the most valuable level available.

## Upgrade Priority

The order that works for most accounts:

1. **Furnace first, always.** It unlocks the FC ceiling for every other building. A higher Furnace is what *lets* you spend elsewhere.
2. **Troop camps next**, in line with your [troop ratio](/troops/ratios) — your most-trained troop type's camp first. FC-tier camps unlock higher-tier troops, which is most of the combat value of the FC era.
3. **Embassy** to keep rally capacity and prerequisites matched.
4. **Command Centre, Infirmary, War Academy** as they gate or as you have spare resources. These are lower-impact and can lag.

Don't spread thin. One building pushed to a meaningful tier beats six buildings nudged up one sub-level each.

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Minimal Spend

- Refine **every day** from the moment the Lab allows it. This single habit decides your FC-10 timeline more than anything else.
- Bank raw Fire Crystals from Intel Missions, Arena/event shops, and Fire Crystal event chests. Spend Fire Crystal event Embers deliberately — Embers carry between events, so you can save for a chest you actually want.
- Speedups go on the **Furnace and your priority camp** only. Everything else takes base time.
- Don't chase FC10. For most accounts the value-per-day flattens hard in the FC8–FC10 range while the RFC cost explodes. Reaching FC6–FC8 with full troop tiers is a strong, realistic stopping zone.

### Light Spenders

- The second construction queue remains the highest-impact purchase — the FC era's base times are long, and two queues roughly halve the calendar.
- Modest Fire Crystal pack value shows up most when it lets you clear a tier right before a points event (Castle Battle, SvS prep) so the upgrade doubles as event score.

### Heavy Spenders

- Money buys raw Fire Crystals and resources easily, but it **cannot buy past the RFC daily cap**. Even a whale's FC-10 timeline is gated by Lab refining. Level the Crystal Laboratory early and keep it refining — that's the one bottleneck spending can't skip.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Letting the refining queue sit idle.** RFC is capped per day. Every skipped day is permanent lost capacity you'll feel at FC9–FC10.
2. **Spreading Fire Crystals across every building.** The FC level that matters is your Furnace and your main troop camp. Thin spreading leaves you with no meaningful tier anywhere.
3. **Burning speedups on low-priority buildings.** Infirmary and Command Centre rarely justify speedups. Save them for the Furnace and camps.
4. **Ignoring the FC5→FC6 shift.** FC6 looks cheaper in raw FC, which lulls people — but it's where RFC starts. If you haven't been refining, you hit a wall here.
5. **Chasing FC10 as F2P.** The last two tiers cost the most RFC for the least relative gain. Know your stopping point before you grind toward it.

## Quick Reference

- **Furnace to FC10:** 11,610 FC + 870 RFC. Whole base to FC10: ~39,700 FC + ~2,960 RFC.
- **Each tier = four sub-levels.** FC1-1 → FC1-4 → FC2.
- **RFC starts at FC6** and is your real bottleneck — refine daily, starting early.
- **Order:** Furnace → priority troop camp → Embassy → the rest.
- **Three troop camps** each cost the listed Troop Camp amount; **War Academy has no FC1**.
- **F2P sweet spot:** FC6–FC8 with full troop tiers; don't over-grind FC9–FC10.
