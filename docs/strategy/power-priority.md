# Power Priority

::: info
Quick take: The biggest and cheapest power gains are the ones most players ignore — keeping the research queue running, promoting troops to your top tier, and keeping every building level with the Furnace. Gear, charms, heroes, and pets matter, but they come after the free wins. Rank your effort by impact and cost, not by what looks exciting. And hold the big upgrades for State of Power prep so each one pays twice.
:::

## How power actually works

Power is a running total. The game gives a power value to nearly everything you own or research and adds it up. Two things follow from that, and both should shape how you spend.

First, not all power is equal in a fight. Research, troop tiers, gear, and charms raise your power number **and** your real combat stats. Other things — raw building levels, large piles of low-tier troops — raise the number more than they raise your strength. For a friendly power race that is fine. For SvS survival, remember that a strong floor — high troop tiers, deep research, solid gear — beats a big headline number.

Second, some power is permanent and some is not. Research and buildings can never be taken from you. Troops can die. That does not make troops a bad place to put power — they are one of the largest sources — it just means you heal and replace them, and you do not panic when the number dips after a war.

The rest of this page ranks the systems by how much power they add and how cheaply, starting with the gains almost everyone is leaving on the table.

## The big three (do these first)

These hold most of your power number, and they are where the free wins live. A mid-game account chasing power should exhaust these before spending on anything gated.

### 1. Research — the permanent foundation

Research is the single best place to put power. Every node adds power that can never be lost, and the Battle tab converts straight into combat stats — infantry health and defense, marksman attack and lethality. It is almost entirely time-gated rather than money-gated, which makes it the best value in the game for a free or low-spending account.

The one rule that matters: **never let the queue sit idle.** The Research Center runs one project at a time, so your real limit is hours of research per day. An empty queue overnight is power you will never get back. Queue something before you log off, every time.

Inside the tree, keep Tool Enhancement (research speed) and Tooling Up (construction speed) maxed for your level first — they make everything else faster — then push the Battle tab for your main troop types.

Spend profile: free. Costs time and common resources only.

### 2. Troop tier — promote up, then fill capacity

A trained troop's power stat is literally the amount of power it adds to your account, and higher tiers add far more. Raising your troops to your highest available tier is usually the single largest block of power a mid-game account can add quickly.

Two moves, in order:

1. **Promote existing troops up.** Promotion upgrades troops you already have for far less than training fresh ones, and it is the fastest way to raise the power your current army carries.
2. **Train into unused capacity.** Idle camp space is wasted power. Fill it.

When you reach the Fire Crystal era, T11 troops are another large jump on top of T10.

The catch: this is "soft" power — it dies in battle. Keep your infirmary and replenishment in order and treat troop power as something you maintain, not set-and-forget.

Spend profile: free. Costs resources and time.

### 3. Buildings — keep pace with the Furnace

Every building level adds power, and the Furnace gates all of them. Two habits:

- **Push the Furnace.** It is the headline number, it unlocks systems (Chief Gear at 22, Chief Charms at 25, the Fire Crystal track at 30), and it sets your SvS matchmaking band.
- **Keep the rest level with the Furnace.** Buildings lagging behind are free power sitting unclaimed, and several are upgrade requirements for the next Furnace level anyway.

The Fire Crystal era — Furnace 30 onward — is a long, steady power stream. It is slower and more resource-hungry than anything before it, but it never really stops giving.

Spend profile: free, but the most resource- and time-intensive of the big three.

## The mid-game multipliers

Smaller total buckets than the big three, but high value per point and the right place to spend gated materials once the free wins are claimed.

### 4. Chief Gear (Furnace 22)

The best evergreen investment in this group. Chief Gear buffs all of your troops directly — it is not tied to any hero or formation — so it keeps paying off through every roster and meta change.

The move: craft all six pieces first to unlock the three-piece and six-piece set bonuses, then enhance. Lead with Infantry — your frontline absorbs the most — then Marksman, then Lancer, and keep the tiers close together rather than pushing one piece far ahead.

Spend profile: gated by Hardened Alloy and Polishing Solution, from beast hunts, Polar Terror rallies, Crazy Joe, and events. Cheap to accumulate, slow to climb.

### 5. Chief Charms (Furnace 25)

Charms sit inside your Chief Gear — three per piece, eighteen in total — and raise troop Lethality and Health. They are a large late-game power source, and one of the highest-scoring activities in SvS prep, which is the main reason to hold them for the window (see below).

The move: spread levels evenly across all eighteen rather than spiking a few, get everything to level 11 to unlock the material exchange, then push higher. Levels 12 and up also need Jewel Secrets, the rarest charm material.

Spend profile: gated by Charm Guides, Charm Designs, and (late) Jewel Secrets, from Frostfire Mine, Castle Battle, and the Giant Elk pet.

### 6. Heroes — stars and exclusive weapons

Heroes carry real power through their level, star rank, skills, and exclusive weapons, and a developed Mythic hero is a large chunk of the number. But hero power is gated by shards, so the rule is **concentrate, do not spread.** Pour shards into your main combat trio — your infantry lead and your damage carry — rather than leveling everyone a little.

Favor attack and lethality heroes for the fastest combat-power return; do not sink premium upgrade resources into secondary lancers.

Spend profile: gated by hero shards (Lucky Wheel, Hall of Heroes, events). The most spend-sensitive item in this tier.

## Supporting power

Smaller per point than everything above, but worth keeping current — and several are easy to forget once unlocked.

### 7. Hero gear

The heroes' own four gear pieces add hero power and combat stats on top of the hero. Upgrade it on your top heroes only, in step with their star level.

### 8. Pets

Combat pet levels, skills, and quality add power and battle buffs. Level the combat pets you actually field; do not spread thin across pets you never deploy.

### 9. Experts (Dawn Academy)

Experts add power and account-wide buffs, gated by expert sigils. Worth steady progress once unlocked, but it sits behind the bigger levers above.

### 10. VIP

VIP level adds a passive trickle of power plus permanent quality-of-life — more alliance help, faster everything. It climbs on its own through daily play, and is only worth treating as a spend target if you are already buying the VIP track.

## Common mistakes (power left on the table)

For most mid-game accounts the fastest gains are not new purchases — they are leaks. Walk this list:

- **Idle research queue.** The biggest leak in the game. Always have something running.
- **Un-promoted troops.** Low-tier troops sitting in your camps are a pile of unclaimed power. Promote them up.
- **Buildings behind the Furnace.** Free power, and often a requirement for the next Furnace level.
- **Fewer than six Chief Gear pieces.** You are missing the set bonuses until all six exist.
- **Top heroes stuck at low stars** while shards pile up unspent.
- **Charms, pets, and experts untouched** long after you unlocked them.
- **Dumping big upgrades the moment you can afford them,** outside an event window. Same power, half the reward.

## Bank it for SvS

This is the one that costs alliances real points: spending power upgrades the moment they unlock instead of holding them for State of Power prep.

The logic is simple. During SvS prep, the actions that already raise your power are also the highest-scoring tasks on the board — and you would be doing them anyway. Spending them during prep pays twice: the permanent power gain, plus the prep points that decide which state wins the buff and attacks first.

The upgrades worth banking for the prep window:

- Fire Crystal building and research upgrades
- Chief Charm levels
- Chief Gear enhancement
- Hero shard upgrades and Lucky Wheel spins
- Troop training and promotion
- Construction and research speedups

This does not mean freeze your account between events. Keep research running and keep gathering — those are not points-gated. It means that when a charm level, a gear enhancement, or a stack of Fire Crystals is ready and prep is close, hold it for the window. The power lands either way; only the points are time-sensitive.

See the [SvS Overview](/events/svs/overview) and the [SvS Prep Checklist](/events/svs/prep-checklist) for exactly what scores on which day.
