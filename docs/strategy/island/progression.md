# Island Progression

::: info Quick take
Island development is a marathon, not a sprint. The phases below give you a clear path from unlock to endgame — follow them in order and you'll never waste Life Essence on the wrong thing at the wrong time.
:::

## Phase 1 — Unlock and Clear (Furnace 19)

**Goal:** Get the island running and start producing Life Essence.

When you upgrade to Furnace 19, the Dock appears in your city. Build it immediately — your survivors leave on a three-day expedition and discover Daybreak Island automatically.

### First Week Checklist

- Build your first Lumber Camp and start clearing trees
- Build your second Lumber Camp when available
- Start upgrading both Lumber Camps toward Lv10 (each upgrade costs Life Essence)
- Position camps strategically near suspected chest locations
- Begin daily ally assists (3 given, 3 received — do this every day from now on)
- Collect Life Essence at least twice daily to prevent cap waste

### What NOT To Do Yet

Don't spend Essence on decorations until both Lumber Camps are at least Lv5. The income boost from faster camps compounds over time, so front-loading camp upgrades pays off within weeks.

## Phase 2 — Foundation (Lumber Camps Maxed)

**Goal:** Build your initial decoration spread and push toward Tree of Life Lv5.

Once both Lumber Camps are at Lv10, your Essence income is as fast as it's going to get from clearing. Now it's time to start spending.

### Buy Order

1. All available Mythic decorations to Lv1
2. All available Epic decorations to Lv1
3. All available Rare decorations to Lv1
4. Check for combat-buff decorations (Luminary Citadel, Hero's Sanctum, Basic Dock) and prioritize their upgrades

### Key Principles

- Breadth first — more Lv1 decorations gives better Prosperity per Essence than upgrading one decoration higher
- Never buy duplicates of limit-1 decorations
- Combat-buff decorations get upgrade priority over cosmetic ones
- Keep collecting Essence twice daily and doing ally assists

### Milestone: Tree of Life Lv5

When your accumulated Prosperity pushes Tree of Life to Lv5, you gain a permanent Troops Attack buff. This is your first major Island payoff. Celebrate, then keep going.

## Phase 3 — Optimization (Tree of Life Lv5–10)

**Goal:** Push to Tree of Life Lv10 and begin upgrading combat decorations.

At this point you've bought most of the available Lv1 decorations. The Prosperity math shifts — now upgrading your best combat decorations provides better returns than buying more low-tier cosmetic pieces.

### Upgrade Priorities

1. **Luminary Citadel** — each level increases the Troops Attack buff. Upgrade copies as you earn them from Labyrinth
2. **Hero's Sanctum** — upgrade for Troops Defense
3. **Basic Dock** — upgrade for Deployment Capacity
4. Continue buying any new Mythic/Epic decorations that become available (events, limited releases)

### Labyrinth Runs

Labyrinth is your primary source for Luminary Citadel and Hero's Sanctum copies. Don't skip Labyrinth events even when rewards feel small — each copy you earn feeds directly into your strongest decoration upgrades.

### Milestone: Tree of Life Lv10

Lv10 grants a permanent Troops Lethality buff and unlocks the Starry Lighthouse blueprint (50,000 Life Essence). This is the second major combat breakpoint.

## Phase 4 — Endgame (Tree of Life Lv10+)

**Goal:** Starry Lighthouse development and maximum combat-decoration levels.

### Starry Lighthouse

Purchase the blueprint for 50,000 Essence when you reach Lv10. The Lighthouse auto-places on your coastline and provides multiple stacking buffs as you upgrade it.

The hard gate is General Accessory Construction Contracts:
- 1 free contract per week
- Additional contracts from State of Power and King of Icefield event rewards
- 10 contracts = 1 Starry Lighthouse Blueprint
- Multiple Blueprints per upgrade level

This is a months-long project. Accept the pace — contract accumulation can't be accelerated with spending beyond event participation.

### Ongoing Priorities

- Continue upgrading Luminary Citadel and Hero's Sanctum as copies arrive
- Upgrade Starry Lighthouse whenever you accumulate enough Blueprints
- Buy new event/limited decorations when offered (they often have above-average Prosperity)
- Keep ally assists going — the daily routine never stops paying off

## Daily Routine (All Phases)

This takes under 60 seconds per day and should become muscle memory:

1. Open Daybreak Island
2. Collect accumulated Life Essence
3. Send and receive ally assists (3 each)
4. Check if any decoration upgrades or purchases are ready
5. Return to main city

If you play morning and evening, split the Essence collection across both sessions to avoid capping.

## Timeline Expectations

Island development is deliberately slow. Rough benchmarks for active players:

| Milestone | Estimated Time |
|-----------|---------------|
| Both Lumber Camps to Lv10 | 2–4 weeks after unlock |
| Tree of Life Lv5 | 2–3 months |
| All trees cleared / Timbermill unlocked | 3–5 months |
| Tree of Life Lv10 | 5–8 months |
| Starry Lighthouse meaningful levels | 8–12+ months |

These are rough estimates that vary based on event participation, Labyrinth luck, and how consistently you collect Essence. The point isn't speed — it's steady progress with no wasted resources.
