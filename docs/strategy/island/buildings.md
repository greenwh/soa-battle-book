# Island Building Priorities

::: info Quick take
Upgrade Lumber Camps to max first (faster Essence production), then pour everything into Tree of Life levels. The Starry Lighthouse is endgame content gated behind Tree of Life Lv10 and weekly contract limits.
:::

## Tree of Life

The Tree of Life is the central structure on Daybreak Island. Its level determines your global stat buffs and gates access to advanced decorations and features.

### Key Milestone Buffs

| Level | Buff |
|-------|------|
| 5 | +Troops Attack |
| 10 | +Troops Lethality |
| Various | Healing Speed, Troop Capacity, Defense at intermediate levels |

Tree of Life levels are unlocked by accumulating Prosperity, which comes from placing and upgrading decorations. You can't rush this with spending alone — it's a steady grind of buying the right decorations and leveling them efficiently.

**Priority target:** Level 5 first (Attack buff), then push to Level 10 (Lethality buff). Everything between is valuable but these two are the major combat breakpoints.

## Lumber Camps

You start with one Lumber Camp and can build a second. Each can be upgraded to level 10 using Life Essence, with each level increasing tree-clearing speed.

**Upgrade both Lumber Camps to max as early as possible.** Faster clearing means faster Essence generation, and clearing trees also reveals hidden chests with bonus rewards.

### Clearing Strategy

Lumber Camps clear trees in the direction of nearby forest. You can influence which areas get cleared first by positioning your camps strategically:

- Place camps near areas where you suspect hidden chests (community layouts show common chest locations)
- Use decorations like water features to block paths and funnel workers toward specific areas
- Workers clear faster when you're on the Daybreak Island screen (online efficiency is higher than offline)

Once all trees are cleared, Lumber Camps become normal decorations and you unlock the Timbermill.

## Timbermill

The Timbermill replaces your Lumber Camps as the Life Essence production building after all trees are cleared. It produces Essence continuously without needing trees.

This is a milestone, not a decision point — it appears automatically when clearing is complete. The transition is seamless; your Essence income continues without interruption.

## Starry Lighthouse

Unlocked at Tree of Life Level 10, the Starry Lighthouse is a unique decoration purchased for 50,000 Life Essence. It provides multiple stacking buffs and can be upgraded to level 10.

### The Catch: General Accessory Construction Contracts

Unlike other decorations, upgrading the Starry Lighthouse requires Blueprints, which are purchased with General Accessory Construction Contracts. These contracts are hard-gated:

- 1 free contract per week (refreshes weekly)
- Additional contracts available as rewards from State of Power and King of Icefield events
- 10 contracts = 1 Blueprint
- Multiple Blueprints needed per level

This makes the Starry Lighthouse a true long-term project. F2P players should plan for months of contract accumulation. Don't stress about it early — focus on getting Tree of Life to 10 first, then start the Lighthouse grind.

## Upgrade Priority Order

1. **Both Lumber Camps → Lv10** (accelerates everything else)
2. **Combat decorations → Lv1** (see [Decoration Strategy](/strategy/island/decorations))
3. **Tree of Life → Lv5** (Attack buff milestone)
4. **Combat decorations → higher levels** (after Lv5 milestone)
5. **Tree of Life → Lv10** (Lethality buff milestone)
6. **Starry Lighthouse** (endgame, contract-gated)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Upgrading Tree of Life before Lumber Camps.** The Tree levels itself through Prosperity from decorations — you can't directly spend Essence on it. But Lumber Camp levels directly increase your Essence income, which lets you buy more decorations faster.

2. **Ignoring worker positioning.** Just plopping Lumber Camps down randomly means slower clearing and missed chests. A few minutes of layout planning pays off.

3. **Rushing the Starry Lighthouse.** It's gated by weekly contracts regardless of how much Essence you have. Accept the pace and focus on what you can control.
