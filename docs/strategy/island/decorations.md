# Decoration Strategy

::: info Quick take
Buy level 1 of every Mythic decoration first (best Prosperity per Essence), then Epic, then Rare. Focus upgrades on combat-buff decorations before cosmetic ones. Never buy duplicates of decorations with a limit of 1.
:::

## How Decorations Work

Decorations are the core progression mechanic on Daybreak Island. Each decoration you place provides two things: **Prosperity** (which levels your Tree of Life) and often a **stat buff** (which directly improves your combat performance).

Decorations are purchased from the Marketplace using Life Essence, and many can be upgraded to higher levels for increased Prosperity and stronger buffs.

## Rarity Tiers

Decorations come in five rarity tiers. The efficiency of Prosperity gain per Life Essence spent varies significantly:

| Rarity | Prosperity Efficiency | Buff Type |
|--------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Basic** | Lowest — cosmetic only | No buffs, no upgrades |
| **Uncommon** | Low | Minor utility buffs |
| **Rare** | Moderate | Utility and minor combat |
| **Epic** | Good | Combat-relevant buffs |
| **Mythic** | Best return per Essence | Strongest combat buffs |

**Key insight:** Building level 1 of a Mythic decoration gives more Prosperity per Essence spent than upgrading a lower-rarity decoration. This is why the optimal strategy is to buy all available Mythic Lv1 decorations before upgrading anything.

### Buy Order

1. All Mythic decorations to Lv1
2. All Epic decorations to Lv1
3. All Rare decorations to Lv1
4. Then upgrade combat-buff decorations in priority order (see below)

After reaching Tree of Life Lv10, the upgrade focus shifts — at that point, leveling your best decorations higher becomes more valuable than buying new low-tier ones.

## Combat-Relevant Decorations

Not all decorations provide combat buffs. Prioritize these:

| Decoration | Max Level | Buff | Source |
|-----------|-----------|------|--------|
| **Luminary Citadel** | 10 | +10% Troops Attack (at max) | Labyrinth rewards |
| **Hero's Sanctum** | 5 | +2.5% Troops Defense (at max) | Labyrinth rewards |
| **Basic Dock** | 10 | +1,000 Deployment Capacity (at max) | Marketplace |
| **Barbecue Stand** | Max | +5% Training Speed | Marketplace |
| **Starry Lighthouse** | 10 | Multiple buffs (Attack, Lethality, Defense) | Tree of Life Lv10 unlock |
| **Type-specific decorations** | Varies | +Attack or +Defense for specific troop types | Various sources |

### Priority Upgrades

After buying your initial spread of Lv1 decorations:

1. **Luminary Citadel** — +10% Troops Attack at max level is the single strongest decoration buff. Upgrade copies as you earn them from Labyrinth.
2. **Hero's Sanctum** — +2.5% Troops Defense. Smaller numbers than Citadel but Defense is harder to stack elsewhere.
3. **Basic Dock** — Deployment Capacity directly increases how many troops you field. More relevant for rally leaders and garrison commanders.
4. **Starry Lighthouse** — Endgame priority. Contract-gated, so upgrades happen at their own pace regardless of Essence.

## Prosperity Math

Prosperity is not proportional to decoration level. Two key rules:

**Two cheap decorations beat one expensive one.** Two 1,000-Essence decorations provide more combined Prosperity (and two achievement counts) than one 2,000-Essence decoration. When choosing what to buy next, breadth first.

**Limit-1 decorations don't stack.** If a decoration has a "limit 1" restriction, buying a second copy provides zero additional Prosperity. Check the limit tag before purchasing. Diversify your decoration portfolio instead.

**3,000-Essence decorations are the sweet spot.** Community testing indicates that decorations in this price range provide the best Prosperity-per-Essence ratio for their tier.

## Event and Limited Decorations

Some decorations are only available during specific events or as limited-time rewards. These typically provide above-average Prosperity and are worth acquiring when offered. However, they follow the same combat-buff priority: if a limited decoration is cosmetic-only, it's lower priority than a permanent combat decoration.

## Recycling

Unwanted decorations can be sold at the Recycle Shop for partial Life Essence refund. The refund is not 100% — expect to lose some Essence in the process. Only recycle decorations you're certain you don't need, and never recycle combat-buff decorations.

::: warning
High-level decorations can be dismantled into lower-level copies. This is irreversible and rarely worthwhile. Don't tap the dismantle button unless you're absolutely sure.
:::

## Common Mistakes

1. **Upgrading one decoration to max before buying others.** Breadth beats depth for Prosperity until you hit Tree of Life Lv10.

2. **Ignoring Labyrinth rewards.** Luminary Citadel and Hero's Sanctum copies come from Labyrinth — don't skip Labyrinth runs even if the event feels tedious.

3. **Buying Basic decorations.** They look nice but provide zero buffs and minimal Prosperity. Save your Essence for Rare+ decorations.

4. **Recycling combat decorations to fund cosmetic ones.** Never trade stats for aesthetics.
