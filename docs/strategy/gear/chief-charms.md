# Chief Charms

::: info
Quick take: Charms are the Furnace 25 layer that adds **Lethality and Health** to all your troops. They matter more than their size suggests, because Lethality is the one stat the rest of your account barely provides. Level Chief Gear quality first, push one charm to Level 11 to unlock the material exchange, then spread levels across all eighteen toward the troop types you actually field. Charms are also one of the highest-scoring SvS prep tasks — hold them for the window.
:::

## What charms are

Chief Charms unlock at **Furnace 25** and attach to your Chief Gear. Each of your six gear pieces has **three charm slots**, for **eighteen charms** in total. A charm takes its troop type from the gear piece it sits on, and you cannot change it. Unlike Chief Gear, charms have **no set bonus**, so you are free to level them in any order.

What they give: **Troop Lethality and Health** for one troop type per charm, applied to all your marches automatically.

## Why charms punch above their weight

Your troop damage is roughly **Attack multiplied by Lethality.** The rest of your account — research, heroes, buildings, Chief Gear — pours in plenty of Attack and Defense, but very little Lethality and Health. Charms are the main source of those two scarce stats, so a level of charm often buys more real combat power than the same effort spent on a stat you already have in surplus. That is the case for not treating charms as an afterthought once they unlock.

## The slot map

Charms inherit their troop type from the gear piece, matching your [Chief Gear](/strategy/gear/chief-gear) layout:

| Troop Type | Gear Pieces | Charms |
| ---------- | ----------- | ------ |
| **Infantry** | Coat + Pants | 6 (Health & Lethality) |
| **Marksman** | Ring + Weapon (Cane) | 6 (Health & Lethality) |
| **Lancer** | Cap (Helmet) + Watch | 6 (Health & Lethality) |

## How leveling works

Charms go from **Level 1 to 16.** From Level 4 on, each major level is split into sub-levels you clear in sequence:

- Levels 4–10: four sub-levels each (4 → 4.1 → 4.2 → 4.3 → 5)
- Levels 11–16: five sub-levels each (10 → 10.1 → 10.2 → 10.3 → 10.4 → 11)

Two breakpoints matter:

- **Level 11** unlocks the **Chief Charm Material Exchange** the moment any single charm reaches it. The exchange lets you trade surplus materials for the ones you are short on, which speeds up every charm after.
- **Levels 12–16** require **Jewel Secrets**, the rarest charm material, and the top level (16) is gated behind your state reaching **Generation 7.** Below Gen 7 the cap is Level 15.

## Upgrade strategy

1. **Gear quality first.** Charm bonuses scale with the quality of the gear they sit on, so a solid Chief Gear floor makes every charm worth more. If you are still crafting gear pieces, do that first — but if you have charm materials sitting idle while you wait on gear materials, spend them, since the two use completely separate resources.
2. **Push one charm to Level 11 early** to unlock the material exchange. After that, balancing everything else gets much easier.
3. **Then spread across the eighteen**, weighted to the troops you actually field. There is no set bonus rewarding uniformity, so match your fielded ratio: a marksman-line account leans marksman charms first (lethality is your damage); an infantry-heavy PvP or Alliance Championship build leans infantry charms first (survivability). Do not pour materials into a troop type you barely deploy.
4. **Level 11 across all eighteen is the natural free-to-play plateau** — it is the last tier before Jewel Secrets. Past that, push to 12+ only as Jewel Secrets allow.

A balancing tip: you do not have to win every stat through charms. If your charms lean infantry, you can lean your [research](/strategy/research) or hero gear toward marksman, so your overall stats end up balanced relative to what you field.

## Materials

| Material | Role | Main sources |
| -------- | ---- | ------------ |
| **Charm Guides** | Common; needed at every level (large amounts late) | Frostfire Mine, Castle Battle, Foundry / Power Region / Canyon shops, events |
| **Charm Designs** | The real bottleneck; needed at every level | Frostfire Mine, Castle Battle, the same shops, and the **Giant Elk pet** via Lighthouse Intel Missions |
| **Jewel Secrets** | Rare; only for Levels 12–16 | Frostfire Mine, Castle Battle, high-tier event and shop rewards |

The [Frostfire Mine](/events/frostfire-mine) and Castle Battle ([Sunfire Castle](/events/sunfire-castle)) are your steady supply. If you are pushing charms seriously, the Giant Elk pet is worth investing in for its passive Charm Design income.

## Bank it for SvS

Charm upgrades are one of the highest-scoring tasks in State of Power prep, and the points climb sharply at higher charm levels. That makes charms a prime thing to **bank**: when you have a charm level ready and prep is close, hold it for the window. You get the permanent Lethality and Health either way — and during prep you also get the points that decide which state wins the buff and attacks first. See the [SvS Prep Checklist](/events/svs/prep-checklist) for the scoring days.

This is the discipline that wins states: not hoarding charm materials forever, but timing the **spend** to land inside prep.

## Common mistakes

- **Spiking one troop type to the ceiling** while the others sit at Level 1. Spread to match what you field.
- **Maxing charms on low-quality gear.** The charm scales with gear quality — raise the gear floor so the charms pay off.
- **Never reaching Level 11 on any charm,** so the material exchange stays locked and your progression stays slow.
- **Dumping charm levels the moment you can afford them,** outside an SvS window. Same stats, but you throw away the prep points.

## Quick reference

- Unlocks at Furnace 25 · 18 charms · 3 per gear piece · troop type fixed by slot · no set bonus.
- Charms give Lethality and Health — the stats nothing else provides much of. High value per resource.
- Gear quality first; charm bonus scales with it.
- Get one charm to Level 11 to unlock the material exchange, then spread.
- Level 11 across all eighteen is the F2P plateau; 12–16 need Jewel Secrets (16 needs Gen 7).
- Weight charms to the troops you field; balance other systems to cover the rest.
- Bank charm levels for SvS prep — top scorer, and you would spend them anyway.
