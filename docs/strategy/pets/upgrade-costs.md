# Pet Upgrade Costs

::: info Quick take
Pets level with **Pet Food** and advance every 10 levels using **Taming Manuals** (plus **Energizing Potions** from Level 30 and **Strengthening Serums** from Level 50). Cost scales with the pet's rarity tier — higher tiers cost more per level but reach a higher cap, up to Level 100. Pour food into pets you will keep; don't spread it thin.
:::

## How Pet Upgrading Works

Pets unlock at Furnace 18 through the Beast Cage. There are five rarity tiers, and a pet's rarity sets its maximum level: the lowest tier caps at Level 50, the highest at Level 100.

Two costs drive pet growth:

- **Leveling** uses Pet Food. Each level adds passive Troop Attack and Defense. Higher-rarity pets cost more food per level.
- **Advancement** happens at every 10th level (10, 20, 30, …). It raises the level cap, upgrades the pet's skill, and costs advancement materials: **Taming Manuals** at every milestone, **Energizing Potions** from the Level 30 advancement on, and **Strengthening Serums** from the Level 50 advancement on.

Each advancement also adds **Pet Advancement Score**, which feeds your SvS Day 3 and Day 5 totals — so timing advancements for an SvS window double-dips as event score.

Refinement (improving a pet's stat *quality* with Wild Marks) is a separate system with its own costs and rules — see [Combat Pets](/strategy/pets/combat-pets).

## Material Sources

- **Pet Food** — Pet-related events, daily activity, the Beast Cage's own production, and packs.
- **Taming Manuals, Energizing Potions, Strengthening Serums** — pet events, event shops, and packs. These are the gate on advancement, not Pet Food, at higher tiers.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Spreading Pet Food across every pet.** Food is the cheap part early but adds up fast at higher tiers. Concentrate it on the pets you actually field.
2. **Leveling past a cap you can't advance.** A pet stops at its cap until you advance it. If you're short on manuals or potions, leveling food into a capped pet does nothing — bank the materials first.
3. **Skipping advancements before SvS.** Advancements score in SvS Day 3 and Day 5. Holding a ready advancement for the SvS window turns a routine upgrade into free event points.
4. **Confusing advancement with refinement.** Advancement (manuals/potions/serums) raises level and skill. Refinement (Wild Marks) changes stat quality. They're separate spends — see [Combat Pets](/strategy/pets/combat-pets) for refinement discipline.

## Quick Reference

- **Leveling = Pet Food. Advancement (every 10 levels) = Manuals, then Potions (Lv30+), then Serums (Lv50+).**
- **Higher rarity = higher cost per level + higher cap** (50 / 60 / 70 / 80 / 100).
- **Advancements score in SvS Day 3 and Day 5** — time them for the window.
- **Manuals/potions/serums are the real gate** at higher tiers, not food.
- **Refinement is separate** — see Combat Pets.
