# Combat Pets

::: info Quick take
Eight combat pets provide 2-hour active skill buffs for competitive events. Cave Lion (+10% Attack) and Saber-Tooth Tiger (+10% Lethality) are S-tier and should be leveled first. Snow Leopard is the only combat pet not yet unlocked on Gen 2 servers.
:::

**Server status (Gen 2):** 7 of 8 combat pets unlocked through Titan Roc. Snow Leopard is the remaining combat pet not yet available.

## Leveling Priority

Level combat pets in tier order:

1. **Cave Lion** → max. +10% Attack is the single strongest pet buff. Always activated.
2. **Saber-Tooth Tiger** → max. +10% Lethality is S-tier offense alongside Cave Lion.
3. **Frost Gorilla** → max. +10% Health is the best survivability buff.
4. **Mammoth** and **Snow Ape** in parallel. Defense and deployment — both are A-tier situational.
5. **Iron Rhino** — only if you're a rally leader. +150,000 Rally Capacity is meaningless for joiners.
6. **Titan Roc** — useful but be careful in Bear Trap (see warning below).
7. **Snow Leopard** — when available. B-tier, march speed is situational.

## Refinement Strategy

Pet refinement provides passive stat bonuses (Troop Lethality and Troop Health) using Wild Marks. The process is RNG-based — you spend marks and hope for good stat rolls. Target purple-tier stats minimum.

**The hard rule: save ALL Advanced Wild Marks for S-tier combat pets.**

| Wild Mark Type | Use On |
|---|---|
| Common Wild Marks | Gen 1 utility pets (Cave Hyena, Musk Ox, Arctic Wolf) |
| Advanced Wild Marks | Cave Lion, Saber-Tooth Tiger, Frost Gorilla ONLY |

Advanced Wild Marks are scarce and refinement mats are **NOT recoverable**. Using Advanced marks on a Gen 1 utility pet is one of the most expensive mistakes in the game. You can't undo it.

On strict F2P accounts, this discipline matters even more — every Advanced Wild Mark counts.

## Titan Roc Bear Trap Warning

Titan Roc's Armor Rift (-5% Enemy HP) is useful in PvP but can hurt you in Bear Trap.

**If your alliance reliably kills the bear with time remaining → do NOT use Armor Rift.** It lowers the bear's maximum HP, which reduces your alliance's total damage score, which reduces everyone's rewards.

**If your alliance does NOT kill the bear in time → Armor Rift is safe.** Reducing the bear's HP helps you get closer to a kill.

This is a per-alliance decision. Strong alliances should disable Titan Roc for Bear Trap. Developing alliances can use it freely.

## Activation Routine

Combat pet skills last 2 hours with approximately 20-hour cooldown. Time them for event windows.

**Standard offensive activation (most events):**
1. Cave Lion (Feral Anthem)
2. Saber-Tooth Tiger (Apex Assault)
3. Snow Ape (Tumbling Power) — for rally events

**Standard defensive activation (garrison duty, defending):**
1. Frost Gorilla (Earthbound Vigor)
2. Mammoth (Hardened Skin)

**Rally leader activation:**
1. Cave Lion + Saber-Tooth Tiger + Snow Ape (offensive)
2. Iron Rhino (Rallying Beast) — increased rally capacity

See [Pre-Battle Checklist](/combat/pre-battle-checklist) for the complete activation sequence including gem buffs and troop ratios.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Wasting Advanced Wild Marks on utility pets.** Common marks are fine for Gen 1 pets. Save Advanced for S-tier combat pets.

2. **Not leveling combat pets at all.** Pet buffs are free, permanent stat layers that multiply with everything else. Ignoring them is leaving free power on the table.

3. **Activating pet skills at the wrong time.** 2-hour duration means activation 3 hours before an event is wasted. Time it for the event window.

4. **Using Titan Roc Armor Rift in Bear Trap when your alliance kills the bear.** Alliance-wide punishment for one player's mistake.

5. **Not activating pets before AC registration.** Alliance Championship snapshots buffs at registration. See [Pet Buffs](/combat/pet-buffs) for the full explanation.
