# Canyon Clash

::: tip
**Quick take:** Monthly cross-state three-way alliance battle. Top 20 alliances on the power leaderboard only. Six-day schedule (vote → register → match → battle Saturday). One-hour battle in the Lost Canyon map across three stages: Seize & Conquer, Fortress Occupation, and Citadel Onslaught. Fuel is the most important resource. Voice chat coordination is mandatory at competitive levels — this is the most coordination-heavy event in the game.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Duration:** 6-day event cycle, 1-hour battle on Saturday
- **Eligibility:** Members of one of the top 20 alliances on the state's Power Leaderboard before the event starts. Furnace Lv. 16+, joined alliance before registration, active within last 5 days
- **Why it matters:** Refined Fire Crystals (added April 2026), Shotchits for the Deadshot mini-game (added October 2025), Charm Designs, Charm Guides, gems, large resource bundles. One of the highest-skill-cap events in the game.

## How It Works

Three alliances enter the Lost Canyon map and fight for control of buildings, fortresses, and the Frozen Citadel. Each alliance has its own territory ("island") with a Bastion (deployment point), neutral buildings, and a fortress. Bridges and airports connect the territories.

**Why it's distinctive:**

- **Three-way warfare.** Not two states. Three alliances simultaneously contest the map.
- **No rallies.** All combat is automated 1v1 building battles. You queue squads into a contested building; squads fight one at a time.
- **Troops don't permanently die.** Defeated squads return to your Bastion, heal over time, or instantly revive with Fuel.
- **Fuel limits everything.** Deploying, advancing, retreating, conscripting, and instantly reviving all consume Fuel.
- **Voice chat is essentially mandatory.** Typing in alliance chat is too slow for the pace of the event.

### The 6-Day Cycle

| Day | Phase | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Voting | Alliance votes on preferred timezone for Saturday battle |
| Tuesday | Voting (continues) | Vote tally finalizes |
| Wednesday | Registration | R4/R5 register members for Legion 1 (and Legion 2 if eligible) |
| Thursday | Registration (continues) | Final roster locks at end of day |
| Friday | Matchmaking | System pairs three alliances of similar power |
| **Saturday** | **Battle Day** | **1-hour battle in Lost Canyon** |

### Eligibility Details

- Top 20 alliances on the **Power Leaderboard** before the event begins
- **Legion 1 unlocks by default;** Legion 2 requires Alliance Lv. 6+ and 40 members logged in this week
- Each Legion needs **at least 15 combatants + substitutes** to qualify for matchmaking
- Members must have joined the alliance **before registration starts**
- Members offline for **5+ days** cannot be dispatched (already-dispatched members keep their slots)

### The Battle Map: Lost Canyon

Three alliance territories ("islands") arranged in a triangle. The Frozen Citadel sits in the center.

| Building Type | Role |
|---|---|
| **Bastion** | Your alliance's deployment point. Where defeated squads return. |
| **Neutral Buildings** | Capture for points. Spread across the map. |
| **Airports / Ports / Gates** | Connect territories. Required to invade enemy land or reach the Citadel. 6 per alliance. |
| **Fortresses (Keeps)** | High-value chokepoints. One per alliance territory. Open in Stage 2. |
| **Frozen Citadel** | The center prize. Opens in Stage 3. ~50,000 bonus points + ongoing point generation. |

### Fuel (The Critical Resource)

Fuel powers everything. Without Fuel, you can't move troops, heal, retreat, or revive.

**Fuel sources:**
- Natural regeneration over time
- **Guardian appointment** — R4/R5 can appoint a player as Guardian of a captured building, accelerating that player's Fuel income
- A player can be Guardian of one building at a time
- Players can appoint themselves as Guardian of a building they're garrisoning

**Fuel consumption:**
- Deploying squads (cost depends on march length and squad size)
- Conscripting troops (cheap healing inside captured buildings)
- Instant revive (expensive — use sparingly)
- Retreating mid-march
- Advancing across long distances

::: warning Fuel discipline wins matches
Running out of Fuel in the final 20 minutes means you can't contest the Frozen Citadel. Use **conscription** (cheap healing inside captured buildings) instead of marching squads back to the Bastion for healing. Save instant revives for emergencies only.
:::

### Combat Mechanics

**1v1 automated battles.** When you send a squad to a contested building, it queues. Battles resolve one at a time. The squad that lands the killing blow on the building's last defender captures it.

**This is NOT a rally.** You cannot pile multiple squads onto one fight simultaneously. Squads queue and fight in sequence.

**Healing options:**
- **At Bastion:** Defeated squads return automatically and heal over time (free, but slow)
- **Conscript:** Refill troops directly inside a captured building (low Fuel cost, fast)
- **Instant Revive:** Bring a defeated squad back immediately (high Fuel cost)

**March speed matters.** Speed-up items work and can decide whether you secure a contested building or arrive after another alliance has already captured it.

::: warning March Speed and Deployment Capacity bonuses are disabled
The wiki confirms: "Except For March Speed and Deployment Capacity bonuses, other bonuses are effective on the battlefield." Your settlement's march speed buffs and deployment capacity boosts do **not** apply inside Canyon Clash. Tech research, VIP, items, and skin bonuses **do** apply.
:::

### Building Defenders (Garrisons)

When you capture a building, your squad becomes its garrison. Subsequent enemy attacks must defeat your garrison to take the building. **Defensive expedition skills matter** — pick heroes with defensive skill 1 for garrison squads.

Rally widgets do **not** work in Canyon Clash (no rallies exist). Only **defensive widgets** apply to garrisons.

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Seize & Conquer (Minutes 0–17, after 3-min prep)

**Goal:** Capture as many neutral buildings as possible. Establish presence in your territory.

**Strategy:**
- **Split into three teams.** The map is too large to operate as one group. Three coordinated squads moving in parallel cover ground 3× faster.
- **Recommended split:** Two strong players in each team, with the third team having your strongest player.
- **Capture all buildings on your island first.** Don't push into enemy territory until your home is secure.
- **Push toward bridges/airports near the end of Stage 1.** Establishing position at the bridge before Stage 2 sets up Fortress access.

**Important point ratio:** Most points in Stage 1 come from buildings you control — held passively, generating points over time. The longer you hold them, the more points you accumulate. **Capturing late is far worse than capturing early.**

### Stage 2: Fortress Occupation (Minutes 17–37)

**Goal:** Take and hold the three Fortresses.

**Strategy:**
- Fortresses generate **high points** and serve as **chokepoints** controlling enemy movement
- **Defend your own Fortress at all costs.** Losing it is a major points and positioning loss.
- **Attempt to capture an enemy Fortress** if your home Fortress is secure
- **The middle-lane team should focus primarily on Fortress defense**
- Ensure all airports near crossover bridges are under your control by end of Stage 2

**Communication peak:** Stage 2 has the most chaotic action of the event. Voice chat coordination becomes essential. Expect simultaneous defense calls, raid alerts, and reinforcement requests.

### Stage 3: Citadel Onslaught (Minutes 37–57)

**Goal:** Capture and hold the Frozen Citadel.

**Strategy:**
- **Send your strongest squads to the Citadel 1–2 minutes before it opens** so they're in position
- The first alliance to defeat the final NPC guard inside the Citadel claims it
- **Once captured, the Citadel must be continuously reinforced** to maintain control
- The Citadel awards **~50,000 bonus points** during the Tally Stage plus ongoing point generation while held
- **Don't over-commit to the Citadel.** Keep enough force defending Fortresses and key buildings — losing your home territory while attacking the Citadel is a net loss

**A common mistake:** Alliances that are losing in Stages 1–2 sometimes ignore the rest of the map and throw everything at the Citadel. This can work, but only if your territory has enough passive defenders to prevent collapse during the rush. **The Citadel rewards momentum, not desperation.**

### Tally Stage

- Final scores calculated
- Frozen Citadel bonus points distributed to whoever held it longest
- Personal Merit and Alliance Ranking rewards issued

## Scoring & Rewards

### Personal Merit Rewards

Based on individual performance during the battle. Required threshold: **10,000 Personal Merit points to qualify**.

Rewards include:
- **Refined Fire Crystals** (added April 2026)
- **Shotchits** for the Deadshot mini-game (added October 2025)
- **Charm Designs** for Chief Gear upgrades
- Gems
- Resource bundles

### Alliance Ranking Rewards

Based on **Legion 1's** point ranking at the battle's end (1st, 2nd, or 3rd place).

If your alliance is granted **auto-advance** (no opponent matched), all members automatically receive No. 1 alliance rewards.

Rewards include:
- Charm Guides (rare resource for Chief Gear progression)
- Avatar Frames
- Gems
- Larger resource bundles for higher placements

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Newer Alliance Members

Canyon Clash gating means F2P participation requires being in a top 20 alliance. Once you're in, individual contribution priorities:

1. **Hit 10,000 Personal Merit minimum.** This is the qualifying threshold. Below it, you receive nothing personally.
2. **Listen on voice chat, follow your team lead's commands.** New players who improvise hurt the team. Your top contribution is reliable execution.
3. **Defend buildings rather than attack enemy positions.** Losing your captured building is more costly than failing an attack.
4. **Use conscription for healing instead of instant revive.** Save your Fuel for the Citadel push.

### Active Alliance Member

- **Volunteer for Guardian role** if you're online for the whole battle. Faster Fuel income compounds.
- **Coordinate three-team splits with your R4/R5.** Don't go solo.
- **Capture and HOLD.** Each held building generates passive points. Captured-then-lost is worse than not captured.
- **Be ready for Stage 3 Citadel rush.** Have your strongest 3 squads pre-positioned.

### R4 / R5 Coordinator

- **Pre-Battle planning is everything.** Map review, team assignments, Guardian list, voice chat setup.
- **Vote registration and 5-day-active check.** Make sure all 30 combatants meet eligibility.
- **Set up clear team roles before battle:** Team 1 = which buildings, Team 2 = which buildings, Team 3 = strongest, Citadel-ready.
- **Watch the timer constantly.** Stage transitions matter — Fortresses open at 17:00, Citadel at 37:00. Pre-position before each transition.

### Heavy Spender

- Pre-event troop training packs to maximize squad size
- Speed-up packs for critical mid-battle marches
- Top-tier Hero Gear maximizes 1v1 squad strength
- Personal Merit top placements have meaningful rewards beyond gear materials

## Common Mistakes

1. **Going solo.** Combat is 1v1, but coordination wins matches. A skilled solo player gets eliminated quickly. Stay with your team.

2. **Over-committing to the Citadel.** Yes, it's worth ~50,000 bonus points, but losing your Fortresses and buildings while contesting it is a net negative.

3. **Wasting Fuel on instant revives.** Conscription is cheaper. Bastion healing is free. Save instant revive for actual emergencies.

4. **Trying to rally squads into one fight.** This isn't possible — combat is 1v1 only. Sending 5 squads to one building means they fight in sequence, not together.

5. **Forgetting March Speed bonuses don't work inside.** Your settlement's +50% march speed buff is disabled. Plan march timing accordingly.

6. **Not appointing Guardians.** Untracked buildings without Guardians produce significantly less Fuel income. R4/R5 should track Guardian assignments throughout the battle.

7. **Picking attack-skill heroes for garrison defense.** Defensive Expedition Skill 1 matters for held buildings. Garrison heroes should have defense-oriented first skills.

8. **Failing the 10,000 Personal Merit threshold.** Below this, you get nothing personally regardless of alliance ranking. Make sure every member crosses it.

9. **Spreading forces too thin in Stage 1.** Capturing 15 weak buildings then losing them in Stage 2 is worse than holding 8 buildings consistently.

10. **Leaving the battlefield mid-event.** A 12-minute cooldown blocks re-entry. If you have to step away, plan around the cooldown.

11. **Not using voice chat.** Typing 30 messages per minute during chaotic Stage 2 is impossible. Voice is essentially mandatory at competitive levels.

12. **Picking heroes with rally widgets.** No rallies exist in Canyon Clash. Rally widget bonuses are wasted. Only **defensive widgets** apply to garrisons.
