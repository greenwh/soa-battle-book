# Crazy Joe

::: tip
**Quick take:** 40-minute alliance defense event. 20 waves of AI bandits hit your alliance — most target individual cities, two target the Alliance HQ, three are Elite waves that only attack online players for double points. Shields don't work. The event is one of the best F2P sources of Chief Gear materials (Polishing Solution, Hardened Alloy, Design Plans). Mastering reinforcement timing and Marksman discipline is the difference between mid-tier and top scores.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly schedule — runs Tuesday and Thursday during weeks that feature SvS, KoI, or Hall of Chiefs (so roughly every other week, two days per active week)
- **Duration:** ~40 minutes per event
- **Eligibility:** Furnace Lv. 7+
- **Activation:** R4 or R5 manually starts the event or schedules it; alliance must have at least one HQ built
- **Why it matters:** Polishing Solution, Hardened Alloy, Design Plans (Chief Gear materials), Alliance Tokens, gems. One of the best F2P-friendly sources for Chief Gear progression.

## How It Works

Joe sends 20 waves of AI bandits at your alliance over ~40 minutes. **Shields don't work** — you can't opt out by shielding up. Only two ways to stop being attacked: lose two defenses (system stops sending waves to you) or teleport mid-event (cancels your participation in the current sequence).

You earn points by **defeating the bandits**. Two ways to score:

1. **Defending your own city** — score is based on % of attackers defeated. 100% kill = max points. Even if reinforcements from allies do all the killing, you still earn full points as the city owner.
2. **Reinforcing alliance members** — score is based on the % of attackers your troops killed relative to other reinforcers in that city.

The wave breakdown:

| Waves | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1–6 | All alliance members | Easy waves; mostly trash mobs |
| **7** | **Online members only** | **Elite wave — double points, only targets online** |
| 8–9 | All alliance members | Standard difficulty escalation |
| **10** | **Alliance HQ only** | **HQ wave — coordinated reinforcement required** |
| 11–13 | All alliance members | Difficulty ramps |
| **14** | **Online members only** | **Elite wave — double points** |
| 15–16 | All alliance members | Difficulty spikes (AI troops join the bandit army from Wave 15+) |
| **17** | **Online members only** | **Elite wave — double points** |
| 18–19 | All alliance members | Final escalation |
| **20** | **Alliance HQ only** | **HQ wave — survival focus, heaviest enemy damage** |

The Elite waves (7, 14, 17) are where the points are. "From waves 7, 14, and 17 alone, each online member can earn 229,500 points just from city defense." Missing these waves by being offline is the single biggest individual-score cost in the event.

### Two Defeats and You're Out

If you lose your city defense twice, Joe stops sending waves to you. You can't earn more individual defense points — but you can still earn points by reinforcing allies. **Don't quit when this happens.** Recall all your troops and pivot to reinforcing players who are still in the fight.

### Difficulty Levels

The alliance R4/R5 picks from **21 difficulty levels** at activation. Higher difficulties give better rewards but require more alliance power. Difficulty unlocks progressively — you have to clear the current level and hit point thresholds to unlock the next. Most alliances target a difficulty slightly above their current power.

::: warning Best score is kept
You can run Crazy Joe multiple times across the Tuesday and Thursday windows — only your **best score** is kept for personal milestone rewards. So a poor first attempt isn't a disaster; you can do better on your next run.
:::

## Scoring & Reward Structure

Three reward streams overlap:

### Personal Rewards (Individual Score)

Based on your individual point total ranking in the state. Includes:
- **Polishing Solution** — Chief Gear upgrade material
- **Hardened Alloy** — Chief Gear upgrade material
- **Design Plans** — Chief Gear unlock material
- Resource bundles
- Smaller gem allotments

### Alliance Rewards (Alliance Total)

Based on your alliance's total score vs. other alliances in the state. All members of a top-ranked alliance get tier rewards. Smaller amounts of the same items, but stacks across all members.

### Milestone Rewards (Point Thresholds)

Personal and alliance point thresholds unlock additional rewards (Alliance Tokens primarily). These are not zero-sum with rankings — even mid-tier alliances hit milestones for solid reward stacks.

::: tip Best score is kept across alliance changes
Your highest individual score is preserved even if you switch alliances mid-event-week. So if you score 2 million solo but your alliance only contributed 500k of that, then join a top alliance that scored 60M, you still receive milestone rewards based on your highest individual contribution. Don't be afraid to play hard before joining a stronger alliance.
:::

## Phase by Phase

### Pre-Event (15+ minutes before)

**Train troops if you're not stockpiling for SvS or another event.** Your maximum troop count is your maximum point ceiling. Use any active training boosts.

**Pre-position reinforcements.** As soon as you know who in your alliance is online and active, send marches to them. Troops sent before Wave 1 = points starting from Wave 1.

**The trade-troops trick.** Coordinate with 1–2 specific alliance members to swap reinforcements before the event starts. You send all your Infantry/Lancer to them; they do the same for you. **This roughly doubles both of your scores** because you're now scoring kills in two cities instead of one.

**Activate buffs.** Pet skills, army capacity buffs, troop attack/defense buffs — anything that increases your kill rate. The window is 40 minutes, so 30-minute and 1-hour buffs are perfect timing.

**Check your hero loadout.** Heroes use **Expedition Skills** during city defense. **Set defensive heroes as your Defense Heroes** on the barricade — Sergey, Patrick, Magnus, or other defense-skill leads.

### Event Start — Waves 1–6 (City Defense Mode)

**Send out all Infantry and Lancers to reinforce allies.** Keep only Marksmen and your defense heroes at home. Marksmen are nearly useless for kill counts due to turn-based combat (Infantry engages first, often wipes the wave before Marksmen attack).

**Reinforce online players first.** Each wave attacks all alliance cities, so you'll be defending your own city while your sent troops are scoring kills in others' cities.

**Don't pile too many reinforcements into one city.** When 5 players all reinforce the same target, kills get split too thin and nobody scores well. Spread support across cities that need it most.

### Wave 7 — Elite Wave (Online Players Only)

**Be online.** This wave only attacks online players — being offline costs you ~10% of your potential individual score from this single wave.

**Reinforce online targets.** Sent reinforcement to an offline player during Elite waves earns **zero points**. Confirm targets are online before sending.

### Waves 8–9 — Buildup to HQ

**Continue reinforcement strategy.** Adjust based on which cities are still in (haven't been defeated twice).

**At end of Wave 9, recall everything to your home city.** This is the critical pivot. Wave 10 hits HQ in 60 seconds.

### Wave 10 — Alliance HQ Defense

**The captain (player with highest Rally Capacity) leads the HQ defense.** They need maximum troop count.

**All other members reinforce HQ immediately.** This is one of two waves where everyone converges on the same target.

**Wave 10 troop ratio is debated:**
- **Source A (wos-guide.com):** Wave 10 enemies are weak. Lancers attack faster than Marksmen → ~30% Infantry / 70% Lancer / 0% Marksman to maximize kill speed.
- **Source B (whiteoutsurvival.wiki, ldcloud, multiple guides):** 90% Infantry / 10% Lancer / 0% Marksman because Infantry engages first and lands kills before other troops act.

**The practical answer:** for most alliances, **90% Infantry / 10% Lancer** is safer and more consistent. The 30/70 strategy only outperforms when alliance Lancer power is very high *and* enemies are weak enough for Lancer speed to matter — both conditions need to be true. Default to 90/10/0.

**After the HQ score calculates (1–2 minutes), recall all troops and resume reinforcing allies.**

### Waves 11–13 — Standard Reinforcement

**Resume the trade-troops strategy.** Same logic as Waves 1–6.

### Wave 14 — Elite Wave

Same rules as Wave 7. **Online players only, double points.** Confirm reinforcement targets are online.

### Waves 15–16 — Difficulty Spike

From Wave 15 onwards, AI troops join the bandit army. Damage and kill counts increase significantly. Watch your cities — if you're starting to lose defenses, ask in alliance chat for help.

### Wave 17 — Elite Wave

Same rules. **Online targets only, double points.**

### Waves 18–19 — Pre-HQ Final

**At end of Wave 19, recall everything for HQ Wave 20.**

### Wave 20 — Alliance HQ Defense (Survival Mode)

**Heavy infantry composition.** Wave 20 enemies deal massive damage. "The enemy damage is very high. You must use many Infantry troops to take the damage."

**Default ratio: 90% Infantry / 10% Lancer / 0% Marksman.** No Marksmen — they get smashed without contributing.

**Goal is survival, not kills.** If you can hit 50%+ kill rate while preserving HQ, you've earned the wave. Maximizing kills at the cost of losing HQ is bad math.

**Captain again is the player with highest Rally Capacity.**

### Post-Event (Rewards)

Personal and alliance rewards arrive **the next day at ~10–12 UTC** (so Wednesday from a Tuesday event, Friday from a Thursday event).

Best individual score is preserved across the day's runs.

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Minimal Spend

Crazy Joe is exceptionally F2P-friendly because **you're not spending your stockpile** — you're using existing troops, no resource burn beyond healing later. Three priorities:

1. **Be online for Elite waves (7, 14, 17).** This is non-negotiable for individual score.
2. **Master the trade-troops trick.** Pair with one reliable alliance member every event. Doubles your effective score.
3. **Don't heal during the event.** Healed troops return to your city and reduce kill share for reinforcers. Wait until after Wave 20.

The event scales with troop count, not with spending. F2P alliance members can be top contributors with active play and good coordination.

### Light Spender

Pet refreshes (if Crazy Joe falls during a pet-buff window) and small attack/defense buffs marginally increase kill rate. Not a major investment lane.

### Heavy Spender

Pre-event troop training packs to add another batch of T11/T10 troops before the event. Speedups to heal up between Tuesday and Thursday runs. Optional event reward boost packs add some materials per event but aren't transformative.

The honest answer: Crazy Joe rewards organization and coordination far more than spending. "An organized alliance running difficulty level 15 will outperform a disorganized whale alliance attempting level 21."

## Common Mistakes

1. **Healing troops during the event.** Healed troops return to your city and steal kills from reinforcers, lowering everyone's score. Heal AFTER Wave 20.

2. **Sending Marksmen to reinforce.** Turn-based combat means Marksmen rarely act before the wave is dead. Marksmen contribute almost zero kills. Leave them at home.

3. **Piling 5+ reinforcers into one city.** Kills split thin, nobody scores well. Distribute reinforcements across cities that need help.

4. **Reinforcing offline players during Elite waves.** Elite waves (7, 14, 17) only attack online targets. Sending reinforcements to an offline player = 0 points from that march.

5. **Forgetting to recall before HQ waves.** If your troops are still away in another city when Wave 10 or 20 hits, they're wasted on those waves. Recall at the end of Wave 9 and Wave 19.

6. **Running default troop loadouts (33/33/33).** The game defaults to 33/33/33 ratios. **Manually set 90/10/0** for reinforcement marches.

7. **Quitting after losing your city twice.** "Two defeats and you're out" only stops Joe from attacking *you*. You can still reinforce allies for points until the event ends.

8. **Setting growth heroes (Zinman, Charlie) as Defense Heroes.** Defense heroes use **Expedition Skills** during city defense. Make sure your defense slot is your strongest defensive Expedition skill (Sergey, Patrick, Magnus, etc.) — not a hero whose first skill is for hero growth or non-combat utility.

9. **Letting your alliance R5 pick a difficulty that's too high.** Failed events earn worse rewards than successful events at lower difficulty. "Focus on completing all waves rather than reaching for impossible difficulty tiers you can't sustain."

10. **Trying to attack the spawning bandit camp.** You can't. The camp is invulnerable. Joe only attacks; you only defend.
