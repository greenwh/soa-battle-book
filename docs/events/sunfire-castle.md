# Sunfire Castle Battle

::: tip
**Quick take:** Biweekly state-internal castle battle. All alliances in your state fight each other for control of the Sunfire Castle. Holding it for 2.5 consecutive hours (or longest cumulative hold over the 5-hour battle) wins, and the winning alliance's leader becomes State President. Troops are wounded, not killed — making it one of the most accessible competitive events for participation. The skills and tactics here transfer directly to SvS Castle Battle.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Biweekly (every 2 weeks)
- **Duration:** 5-hour battle
- **Eligibility:** Any player in the state with troops to send. No power tier or alliance ranking restrictions.
- **Why it matters:** State presidency (whoever wins controls state-wide buffs for 2 weeks), Castle Battle Tokens for the in-event shop (Hero Shards, gear materials, gems), Carnage/Occupation/Casualty event points, and direct preparation for SvS Castle Battle which uses identical mechanics

::: warning Two Sunfire Castle events exist
This guide covers the **Domestic** (intra-state) Sunfire Castle Battle that happens every 2 weeks within your own state. There is also an **SvS Sunfire Castle Battle** during State of Power events — same map, same building, but cross-state.

**In both versions, same-state alliances are enemies to each other.** You cannot join allied alliance rallies or reinforce them, even during SvS. The only practical way to participate in either castle battle is to be in the alliance that's leading the attack or defense. See the [SvS Overview](/events/svs/overview) for SvS-specific considerations.
:::

## How It Works

The state holds a 5-hour battle for control of the Sunfire Castle, located in the Sunfire Castle Alert Zone. **All alliances in your state are enemies of each other during this event.** The winning alliance's R5 becomes State President for the next 2 weeks.

### Why it's distinctive

- **Troops don't permanently die.** They become "Severely Injured" and recover via Field Triage after the event. This dramatically lowers the cost of participation.
- **Same-state alliances are enemies.** Unlike SvS, you fight your normal alliance friends here. Sometimes you fight beside friends from other alliances; sometimes you fight against them.
- **Each player can only send one march at a time** to the castle directly. Coordinated rallies and reinforcements work, but solo simultaneous attacks don't.
- **Castle turrets compound over time.** The longer an alliance holds a turret, the stronger its attack becomes against the castle defender.
- **Attendance more than power.** Coordinated participation can defeat a more powerful but disorganized alliance.

### Pre-Battle Setup (2 hours before)

The Sunfire Castle Alert Zone activates **2 hours before the event**. All cities currently within the Alert Zone are randomly teleported.

If you're not in the Alert Zone:
- You can teleport into the Alert Zone starting 1 hour before the event
- Or attack from outside the Alert Zone (longer march distances)
- **Teleporting into the Alert Zone deactivates any active shield permanently** for the duration of the event

The 1-hour pre-event window is when alliance leaders should:
- Confirm rally rotations and reinforcement assignments
- Position teleports near the castle for fast deployment
- Stockpile Healing Speedups (you'll need many)
- Coordinate via voice chat where possible

### Victory Conditions

**Primary win condition:** Hold the Sunfire Castle for **2.5 consecutive hours** during the 5-hour event.

**Fallback:** If no alliance achieves 2.5 consecutive hours, the alliance with the **longest cumulative hold time** wins.

::: warning Patch correction
The **October 22, 2025 patch** changed the rules for all servers from "6-hour battle / 3-hour consecutive hold" to "5-hour battle / 2.5-hour consecutive hold." Older guides on the internet still cite the deprecated values. The 5h/2.5h rule applies to all servers, including S10+.
:::

### The Battlefield

| Building | Role |
|---|---|
| **Sunfire Castle** (center) | The primary objective. Holding it = winning. |
| **Turrets** (2 nearby) | Automatically attack the alliance currently holding the castle. The longer a turret is held by an alliance, the stronger its attack becomes. |

**Turret ownership matters more than most players realize.** A turret held by Alliance A continuously hits Alliance B (which holds the castle) — even if Alliance A isn't directly attacking the castle. Strategic alliances split forces between castle attack/defense and turret control.

### Combat Mechanics

- **Standard rallies allowed** — multiple players join a rally led by a rally captain
- **Reinforcements (garrisons) allowed** — players send troops to friendly castle/turret defenders
- **One march per player at a time to the castle** — but you can simultaneously rally, reinforce, and operate other queues
- **Failed attacks still award event points** — the wiki notes "your goal is to severely injure your troops" because every wounded troop counts toward Casualty Points

**Turret garrison priority:** When defending a turret, send infantry-heavy compositions. Lancers/Marksmen dies first against turret-hitting enemies.

### Scoring System

Three point types:

| Point Type | How earned | Best earned by |
|---|---|---|
| **Carnage Points** | Defeating enemies at the Sunfire Castle and turrets | Active rally leaders, garrison defenders |
| **Occupation Points** | Your troop power while occupying the Sunfire Castle or turrets | Players with strong troop power who hold positions |
| **Casualty Points** | Severely injured or lost troops (yours) | **Anyone** — even failed attacks count |

::: tip The "batch healing loop" technique
The most effective F2P-friendly point-farming technique: attack solo or in rally against an enemy turret in a continuous loop while batch-healing your wounded troops. Each attack injuries troops (Casualty Points), and batch healing in small chunks lets allies' Help action accelerate the heal. This sacrifices resources for points, but maximizes per-hour scoring. Set a resource budget before doing this — it's costly.
:::

## Phase by Phase

### Hour -2 to -1 (Random Teleport Phase)

- Cities in Alert Zone are randomly teleported within it
- Adjust your alliance's coordination plans based on new positions

### Hour -1 to 0 (Active Prep)

- **Teleport into the Alert Zone if you weren't randomly placed there** — but expect your shield to deactivate
- Confirm alliance rally times and assignments
- Distribute Healing Speedups
- Voice chat ready
- Move troops to staging positions near the castle/turrets

### Hour 0 to 1 (Opening Phase)

**Goal:** Establish position, claim turrets, take an early lead on the castle if possible.

- Strong alliances rush the castle at minute 0
- Mid-tier alliances should focus on turret captures (much easier and contributes to attrition)
- F2P/lower-tier players: join rallies, reinforce garrisons, start the batch healing loop for personal points

### Hour 1 to 4 (The Long Haul)

**Goal:** Hold what you've taken. Counter-attack alliances that have control. Maintain consistent damage output.

- **Reinforcement is more important than fresh attacks.** A castle garrison constantly losing troops needs constant reinforcement.
- **Swap heroes between attack and defense modes.** Rally heroes (Jessie, Jeronimo) for attacks; defense heroes (Patrick, Sergey) for garrison.
- **Track the 2.5-hour timer.** If your alliance has been holding for 2 hours and the cumulative hold timer says "leader at 2:15", you're 15 minutes from a major shift. Push reinforcements harder.
- **Watch for the "double rally" attack** described below.

### Hour 4 to 5 (Final Push)

**Goal:** Either lock in your hold, or make a desperation play if you're behind.

- If your alliance has clear cumulative hold lead, **defend at all costs.** Fresh reinforcements every 5 minutes.
- If you're tied or behind, **the double rally** is the standard counter-attack: launch two rallies of similar power in close succession (weakest first). The first kills enemy infantry; the second arrives before reinforcements can heal/replace.
- F2P/casual players continue batch healing loops for personal point maximization.

### Post-Battle

- Winning alliance's R5 becomes State President for 2 weeks
- President can appoint other alliance members as Governor, Minister of Defense, etc.
- Castle Battle Tokens distributed by personal performance
- Field Triage opens — wounded troops auto-recover at lower rates than SvS Field Triage; gem/tome usage helps

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Newer Player

- **Always show up.** Even if you're outclassed, participation = Casualty Points and rewards.
- **Join rallies, don't lead them.** A weak rally lead loses fast; a weak rally joiner contributes their full kill power.
- **Batch healing loop on a turret** is the highest F2P personal point technique.
- **Reinforce friendly garrisons** when not actively attacking.
- **Do not solo-attack the castle** unless you have nothing else to do — solo attacks lose troops without dealing meaningful damage.

### Mid-Tier Player

- **Help your alliance capture and hold turrets.** Turrets compound damage on castle holders, indirectly helping your alliance even when you're not at the castle.
- **Rally captain on turret attacks.** Rally captain earns Occupation and Carnage points; mid-tier players are perfectly suited for turret rallies.
- **Garrison defense duty.** Stable reinforcements are more important than dramatic attacks.

### Whale / Top Player

- **Lead castle rallies.** Your power compounds — you take the castle faster, hold it longer, and your kill counts maximize Carnage Points.
- **Coordinate massive multi-rally pushes** during the final push window.
- **Set the alliance schedule.** Whales who set rally rotation calendars and who's-online schedules dramatically improve alliance performance.

### R5 / Alliance Leader

- **Plan rally rotation 2 hours before event.** Know who's online, who's leading rallies, who's reinforcing.
- **Set up voice chat well in advance.**
- **Coordinate with other alliances if useful.** Some states have informal "weakest alliance gets the help to take it from #1" coordination.
- **The Presidency reward:** if your alliance wins, the R5 becomes President. The President's appointments (Governor, Minister of Defense) are valuable rewards to distribute among alliance members.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Solo attacking the castle.** A single march against a fully-garrisoned castle is wasted troops with no impact. Always join rallies.

2. **Forgetting that shields deactivate when you teleport into the Alert Zone.** If you teleport in, you're committed. Don't teleport in unless you're going to participate.

3. **Sending Marksmen-heavy rallies against turrets.** Turrets prioritize attacking infantry. A 70/20/10 (Inf/Lan/Mar) rally survives turret damage; a 30/30/40 dies fast.

4. **Ignoring turrets to focus exclusively on the castle.** Turrets compound damage on whoever holds the castle. Capturing one turret is often easier than taking the castle, and the strategic value is significant.

5. **No reinforcement plan.** Capturing the castle without a steady reinforcement rotation means you'll lose it within 30 minutes. Plan for sustained defense, not single-burst capture.

6. **Failing to swap to defensive heroes after capture.** Jessie and Jeronimo are great rally leads; Patrick and Sergey are great garrison defenders. Same hero shouldn't do both.

7. **Wasting healing speedups early.** Pace your healing — you'll need much more in the back half of the event than the front half.

8. **Trying to track everything in alliance chat.** Voice chat is essentially mandatory at competitive levels. Typing speed can't keep up with rally coordination.

9. **Not capturing tokens before the shop closes.** Castle Battle Tokens have a redemption window. Spend them in the in-event shop before they expire.

10. **F2P players who skip the event because "they can't compete."** Casualty Points are awarded for *your* troops being wounded — even from failed attacks. Showing up and being useful is the entire bar; you don't need to win the castle to win rewards.
