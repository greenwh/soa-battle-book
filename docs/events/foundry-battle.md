# Foundry Battle

::: tip
**Quick take:** Bi-weekly alliance-vs-alliance battle on a contained map. Two top-20 alliances fight to control 11 buildings across three phases. The Imperial Foundry in Phase 2 is the single highest-value building. Troops don't permanently die — just injured and auto-heal at battle's end. The most coordination-heavy alliance event after Canyon Clash, and where strong organization can beat raw power.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks)
- **Duration:** 1-hour battle, three 15-minute phases (with 15-minute prep beforehand)
- **Eligibility:** Members of one of the top 20 alliances on the Power Leaderboard. Furnace Lv. 16+, joined alliance before registration period ends
- **Why it matters:** Arsenal Tokens (used in the Foundry Shop for Charm Designs, Hero Shards, design plans, gems, resources). Refines into one of the best Chief Gear material sources for active alliance members.

## How It Works

Two alliances are matched and teleported to a contained battlefield map. Each alliance fields up to **two Legions** of 30 combatants + 10 substitutes. Only **Legion 1** affects alliance ranking and rewards. Battle lasts 1 hour, divided into three  phases.

The objective is simple: **earn more Arsenal Points than the enemy alliance by capturing and holding 11 buildings**.

### Why it's distinctive

- **Alliance vs. alliance.** Two-team only, unlike Canyon Clash's three-way warfare.
- **Rallies allowed.** Standard rally mechanics work here, unlike Canyon Clash where combat is 1v1 only.
- **Troops don't permanently die.** They get injured and auto-heal at battle's end (or after 12 min of leaving the battlefield).
- **Healing speedups are critical** — bring a stockpile.
- **Safe Zones exist** on the map where you cannot be attacked.
- **Two phases of point generation:** initial capture bonus + ongoing per-minute occupation bonus.

### The Three-Phase Structure

| Phase | Time | Buildings unlocked |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | First 15 min | Prototype Sites, Repair Facilities, Boiler Room, Central Transit Stations |
| **Phase 2** | Mid 15 min | **Imperial Foundry**, Munitions Warehouse, Mercenary Camp |
| **Phase 3** | Final 30 min | Weapon Workshops spawn randomly across the map |

Every previously-unlocked building remains contestable in subsequent phases. Phase 2 doesn't replace Phase 1 buildings — it adds new ones.

### The Buildings

Verified point values from official wiki and major guides:

| Building | First Capture | Per-Minute Hold | Special Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Imperial Foundry** ⭐ | 9,000 | 1,800 | **Highest-value building.** Centerpiece of Phase 2. |
| **Repair Facilities** (4×) | 3,000 each | 600 each | One in each map corner. Repair foundry machinery. |
| **Prototype Sites** | High | Medium | Second-highest first-capture value. |
| **Munitions Warehouse** | Medium | Medium | **+15% troop damage reduction & boost** for occupier |
| **Mercenary Camp** | 1,200 | 240 | Sends mercenary strike teams against enemy buildings, lowering their offense/defense |
| **Boiler Room** | (must hold 50% of phase) | Phase-end bonus | Buildings need 50% control time to award points |
| **Central Transit Stations** | Medium | Low | **Reduces Advance Teleport cooldown by 50%** |
| **Weapon Workshops** | Spawn-based | Steady | Phase 3 random spawns. Underrated F2P targets. |

**Personal scoring (separate from alliance points):**

- **Offense:** 80 points per 10,000 enemy power defeated
- **Defense:** 40 points per 10,000 enemy power defeated

::: warning Personal points don't contribute to alliance victory
Your Personal Arsenal Points only affect your own ranking and rewards. The alliance battle is decided purely by Alliance Arsenal Points (capturing and holding buildings). It's possible to be MVP on a losing team. Balance personal aggression with alliance objectives.
:::

### Pre-Battle Requirements

When entering the battlefield, all of the following must be true:

- All troops at home (no marching, gathering, scouting, attacking)
- No wounded troops in the infirmary
- No reinforcements stationed in the Embassy
- Auto-Hunt turned off

The game enforces these — you can't enter if conditions aren't met. Check 30 minutes before battle starts.

### Pre-Battle Checks

- **Stock Healing Speedups.** This is the single most-cited preparation tip across all sources.
- **Stock Advanced Teleports.** Mid-battle repositioning is essential.
- **Confirm Legion assignment with R4/R5.** Members can only be in Legion 1 OR Legion 2, not both.
- **Activate buffs that persist.** VIP, tech bonuses, Chief Gear, and skin bonuses carry into battle if active beforehand.
- **March Speed and Deployment Capacity bonuses DO work** in Foundry Battle (unlike Canyon Clash and Frostfire Mine).

## Phase by Phase

### Pre-Battle Prep (15 minutes before launch)

R4/R5 should:

- Assign players to teams (offensive vs defensive split, see Strategy section below)
- Identify primary targets per phase
- Set up voice chat
- Confirm all members meet pre-battle requirements

### Phase 1 — Map Control (Minutes 0–15)

**Goal:** Establish board presence. Capture as many Phase 1 buildings as possible.

**Key targets in priority order:**

1. **Central Transit Stations** — controlling these reduces your alliance's teleport cooldown by 50%, compounding mobility advantage for the rest of the battle. **Capture early.**
2. **Repair Facilities (4 corners)** — solid points (3,000 + 600/min each), four available, lower contention than central buildings.
3. **Prototype Sites** — high first-capture value. Worth contesting.
4. **Boiler Room** — requires 50% control time to award points, so getting here early matters.

**Strategy:**

- **Split into multiple groups.** The map is large; one group can't reach everything in time.
- **Don't let the enemy take ALL Repair Facilities uncontested.** Even if you only hold 2 of 4 corners, that's significant points.
- **Reinforce captured buildings before pushing for the next.** A captured-then-lost building is worse than not captured.

### Phase 2 — Imperial Foundry Push (Minutes 15–30)

**Goal:** Capture and hold the Imperial Foundry. Continue Phase 1 occupation.

**The Imperial Foundry is the centerpiece of the entire match.** 9,000 first-capture points + 1,800/min hold = ~36,000 points if held the full Phase 2 + Phase 3.

**Strategy:**

- **Send your strongest players (whales) to the Imperial Foundry.** This is what "having a whale" matters for in Foundry Battle. Mass rallies are the standard approach — multiple high-power players coordinating their hero loadouts to roll over defenders.
- **Munitions Warehouse is the secondary Phase 2 target.** Its +15% damage reduction & boost compounds with other actions.
- **Mercenary Camp is a force multiplier.** Sends strike teams that lower the defending alliance's offense/defense on a target building. Use it strategically — typically against whatever your alliance is attacking next.
- **Don't lose your Phase 1 captures.** A Phase 1 building held throughout the battle generates 45+ minutes of per-minute points. Letting the enemy reclaim it is a major net loss.

### Phase 3 — Weapon Workshops & Cleanup (Minutes 30–60)

**Goal:** Capture randomly-spawning Weapon Workshops. Hold existing captures. Prepare for the final tally.

**Strategy:**

- **F2P players shine here.** Weapon Workshops are randomly distributed and don't require power to capture. Players who haven't carried Phase 1-2 fights can pick up significant late-game points.
- **Whales should keep defending the Imperial Foundry.** Losing it in the final 10 minutes is devastating.
- **Don't over-extend.** Sending squads to the far corner of the map for one Weapon Workshop while losing your home buildings is bad math.
- **Coordinate via voice chat for the final push.** The most chaotic period of the battle. Teams that don't communicate collapse here.

### Battle End (Minute 60)

- Final scores tallied
- Troops automatically heal
- Personal and Alliance Arsenal Tokens distributed
- Foundry Shop opens with new exchange rates

## Team Split Strategy (For Coordinators)

A common high-performance structure used by competitive alliances:

**Attacking Group (8 strongest players, excluding the top player):**
- Team A1 (4 players): Imperial Foundry capture in Phase 2
- Team A2 (4 players): Phase 1 building captures, then support A1

**Defending Group (top player + 7 remaining):**
- Team D1 (4 players): Defense of captured Phase 1 buildings (Repair Facilities, Transit Stations)
- Team D2 (4 players): Defense of Imperial Foundry once captured

**Why this split:**

- Top player on defense ensures the most-targeted building (Imperial Foundry) has the strongest defender
- Attack/defense split prevents the typical mistake of "everyone attacks, nothing defended"
- Phase 1: A2 helps D1+D2 secure initial buildings, then transitions to attack mode
- Phase 2: A1+A2 hits Imperial Foundry, D1+D2 holds the line
- Phase 3: All groups respond to Weapon Workshops while D2 stays glued to Imperial Foundry

This isn't the only valid structure, but it's the most-cited high-performance approach.

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Newer Alliance Members

- **Phase 3 Weapon Workshops are your highest contribution.** Random spawns mean you're not contesting whales for first capture.
- **Defensive garrison roles in Phase 1.** Holding a captured Repair Facility doesn't require enormous power — just discipline.
- **Volunteer for Mercenary Camp operations.** Sending strike teams from a captured Mercenary Camp is meaningful contribution without needing top-tier troops.
- **Stock 50+ Healing Speedups.** This event burns through them.

### Mid-Power Player

- **Support whales in Imperial Foundry rallies.** Joining a whale's rally adds your power to the kill calculation.
- **Anchor a Phase 1 building defense.** A mid-power garrison with good Expedition skills can hold against weaker attackers.
- **Capitalize on personal scoring.** 80 points per 10,000 enemy power defeated on offense — actively attacking enemy garrisons is high personal value.

### Whale / Top Player

- **Lead Imperial Foundry assaults.** This is your tier-defining role.
- **Set up massive rallies in Phase 2.** Coordinate hero loadouts (mass rally heroes, expedition skill stacks) for maximum kill power.
- **Stick on defense in Phase 3** if you're the top player. Imperial Foundry defense is the highest-value role.
- **Don't waste yourself on Phase 1 grunt work.** Repair Facilities are for mid/F2P.

### R4 / R5 Coordinator

- **Pre-battle assignments are everything.** Every member should know their team, their phase priorities, and who to follow.
- **Voice chat setup before Phase 1.** Don't try to organize once chaos starts.
- **Track Healing Speedup usage.** If members run out, they need to leave the battlefield (12-min cooldown) for free healing.
- **Substitute management.** If a main combatant goes offline mid-event, the substitute system kicks in. Make sure substitutes are online and ready.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Forgetting to clear infirmary before battle entry.** Wounded troops block entry. Heal them ahead of time.

2. **Auto-Hunt left on.** Same — blocks battle entry. Toggle off in advance.

3. **Personal point obsession.** Players chasing MVP by attacking lone enemies while their alliance loses Imperial Foundry is the most common bad-individual-vs-alliance trade.

4. **Capturing buildings without garrisoning.** A captured-but-undefended building gets reclaimed in minutes. Always leave at least one defender.

5. **Going into Phase 3 with empty Healing Speedup stockpile.** Mid-battle troop wear means later phases need healing more than earlier ones. Pace your speedups.

6. **Ignoring Mercenary Camp.** Players see "1,200 first capture / 240 per minute" and skip it. The strike-team effect compounds your alliance's offensive power for the whole battle.

7. **Imperial Foundry tunnel vision.** Some alliances throw everything at the Foundry and lose all their Phase 1 buildings. The Foundry is the highest-value single building, but it's not >40,000 points if you give up 30,000 elsewhere.

8. **Not stocking Advanced Teleports.** Mid-battle repositioning is essential. Running out means you're stuck wherever you are.

9. **Whale players doing F2P work.** Top players should be at Imperial Foundry, not capturing Weapon Workshops in the final phase.

10. **Letting the 12-minute re-entry cooldown catch you off guard.** If you leave the battlefield to heal, plan it around the cooldown — leaving at minute 35 means you can't return until minute 47, missing most of Phase 3.

## Tundra Arms League

Worth knowing: Foundry Battle has a related but separate tournament event called the **Tundra Arms League** — a 2-week tournament that pits multiple alliances in single-elimination brackets across multiple match days. It uses the same map, building, and phase structure as Foundry Battle, but with different scheduling, registration, and reward systems. **Tundra Arms League legions cannot participate in regular Foundry Battles during the tournament.**

If your alliance is invited to Tundra Arms League, treat it as a higher-stakes Foundry Battle with elimination consequences. The strategies in this guide apply directly.
