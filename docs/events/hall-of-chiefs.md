# Hall of Chiefs

::: tip
**Quick take:** Bi-weekly resource-spending competition for states that haven't yet reached SvS. Six or seven days of themed daily stages, with Jeronimo (Gen 1) or Philly (Gen 2) as the headline hero reward. Once your state completes its first SvS, Hall of Chiefs permanently disappears and is replaced by [King of Icefield](/events/king-of-icefield). The skills, stockpile habits, and daily discipline learned here transfer directly to KoI and SvS Prep.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks)
- **Duration:** 5 days (first ever HoC), 6 days (Gen 1 events 2 and 3), or 7 days (Gen 2 onward)
- **Eligibility:** All players in Generation 1 or Generation 2 states. Once your state completes its first SvS event, HoC is permanently replaced by King of Icefield.
- **Total occurrences:** 3 events in Generation 1, then "several" in Generation 2 until first SvS — typically 6–10 total HoC events across a state's lifetime
- **Why it matters:** Jeronimo or Philly Hero shards (top 10 reward), Mythic Skill Manuals, gems, speedups, Chief Gear materials, large resource bundles. Foundation event for learning daily-theme scoring discipline.

::: warning This event has an expiration date for your state
Hall of Chiefs only exists in pre-SvS states (Generation 1 and 2). After your state's first SvS event, HoC permanently disappears and is replaced by King of Icefield. If your state has already done SvS, this guide is reference for understanding state progression — not a current event you'll see. Players in older states should read the [King of Icefield guide](/events/king-of-icefield) instead.
:::

## How It Works

HoC is a **resource-spending competition** within your state. You don't fight other players — you score points by completing daily themed tasks (upgrading buildings, ascending heroes, training troops, gathering, using Chief Gear materials, etc.). Each day has a different theme; using the right resources on the right day maximizes points.

Hall of Chiefs is the precursor to both **King of Icefield** (which replaces it after SvS) and **SvS Preparation Phase** (which uses the same daily-theme structure plus the Saturday Battle Phase). Mastering HoC builds the muscle memory you'll need for both successor events.

### Generation 1 vs Generation 2

| Aspect | Generation 1 | Generation 2 |
|---|---|---|
| **Total occurrences** | Exactly 3 events | Several (until first SvS) |
| **First HoC duration** | 5 days | n/a (already in Gen 1) |
| **Standard duration** | 6 days | **7 days** |
| **Hero of the Season** | **Jeronimo** (top 10 reward) | **Philly** (top 10 reward) |
| **Stages include Chief Gear?** | Only event #2 and #3 | Yes (all events) |
| **Beast Slay vs Gather** | First HoC has Beast Slay; later events have Gathering | Resource Gathering replaces Beast Slay |

Your state moves from Generation 1 to Generation 2 after the **third Hall of Chiefs event**. From the 4th HoC onward, you're in Gen 2 and Philly is the featured hero.

::: tip Gen 2 format becomes the long-term standard
All Hall of Chiefs events from the **3rd occurrence onward** follow the standard 7-day Generation 2 format described below. The first two events have minor differences (5-day and 6-day variants without certain stages). If you're past your 3rd HoC, expect the 7-day Gen 2 format every time.
:::

## The 7 Daily Themes (Generation 2 Standard)

| Day | Theme | Key scoring activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1** | **Power Boost** | Building upgrades, research, Chief Charm, Construction/Research Speedups, Fire Crystals (when unlocked) |
| **Day 2** | **Hero Development** | Hero ascensions, Lucky Wheel spins, Hero Shards, hero gear materials |
| **Day 3** | **Train Troops** | Train troops, **promote troops** (better points/troop than fresh training), Training Speedups |
| **Day 4** | **Resource Gathering** | Gather meat, wood, coal, iron from map tiles. Use Gathering Speed boosts. |
| **Day 5** | **Power Boost (Hero Gear)** ⭐ | **Hero Gear Essence Stones, Hero Gear Widgets** + standard power activities. Often the highest-point day. |
| **Day 6** | **Chief Gear Upgrade** | Chief Gear score increases, Chief Gear material consumption, Chief Charm upgrades |
| **Day 7** | **Hero Development (Final Sprint)** | Mixed scoring — ascensions, leftover speedups, final pushes |

::: warning Match items to their scoring day
The single most important strategic insight in HoC: **using items on the wrong day scores zero or near-zero points.** Mithril used on Day 1 scores nothing. Hero Shards used on Day 4 score nothing. Plan your stockpile by day, not by what you happen to have when you log in.
:::

## Scoring & Rewards

Three reward streams overlap:

### Daily Milestone Rewards (4 tiers per stage)

Each day has 4 point thresholds. Hitting Tier 4 unlocks the day's best rewards. Milestone rewards include:
- Resources (large bundles)
- Speedups
- Gems
- Mythic Skill Manuals (high-value)
- Hero EXP

**Even non-ranking players should always hit Tier 4** — it's the most reliable reward stream regardless of how you place against other players.

### Stage Ranking (Top 100 in state, daily)

Top 100 chiefs in your state by **that day's points**. Resets daily. Requires **50,000+ points in the current stage** to qualify. Pays out gems, speedups, and resources.

### Total Ranking (Top 100 in state, event end)

Top 100 chiefs in your state by **cumulative points across all days**. Pays out at event end. **Top 10 receive the headline Hero of the Season shards** — Jeronimo (Gen 1) or Philly (Gen 2).

::: warning Top 10 typically requires spending
Reaching the top 10 of Total Ranking — the bracket that earns Hero of the Season shards — almost always requires pack purchases. F2P players consistently report top 100 as achievable, top 30 as stretch-achievable with good preparation, and top 10 as effectively pay-gated. Plan accordingly.
:::

## Phase by Phase

### Pre-Event (the week before)

**Stockpile.** This is the most important week. Save:

- **Fire Crystals + Refined Fire Crystals** if unlocked on your server (Day 1 and Day 5)
- **Construction + Research Speedups** (Day 1)
- **Hero Shards** of any rarity (Day 2 and Day 7)
- **Lucky Wheel chips** (Day 2 — see "Lucky Wheel trick" below)
- **Training Speedups** (Day 3)
- **Lower-tier troops** in queue ready for promotion (Day 3)
- **Stamina Cans** (Day 4 if Beast Slay; otherwise less critical)
- **Hero Gear Essence Stones + Widgets** (Day 5)
- **Chief Gear materials** — Polishing Solution, Hardened Alloy, Design Plans (Day 6)
- **Mass speedup stockpile** for the final day push (Day 7)

::: tip The Lucky Wheel scoring trick
On Hero Development stages (Day 2 and Day 7), each Lucky Wheel spin scores roughly **90,000 points** in Generation 2 events — significantly higher than most other point sources. Save Lucky Chips and Hero Recruitment items specifically for these days. Spinning the wheel during the final Hero Development stage with a stockpile of chips is one of the best ranking pushes available.
:::

### Day 1 — Power Boost

- Use Fire Crystals on building/research upgrades
- Burn Construction and Research Speedups
- Upgrade Chief Charm levels
- Pre-queue any major builds you can complete during this stage for instant points

### Day 2 — Hero Development

- Ascend heroes using saved shards (start with Mythic, then Epic, then Rare)
- **Spin the Lucky Wheel** — highest single-action point return in the event
- Apply hero gear materials if you have any to spare

### Day 3 — Train Troops

- **Promote troops** before training fresh ones (more points per troop)
- Burn all Training Speedups
- T10 / T11 promotion (if available on your server) gives the highest rates
- Pre-queue large training batches the day before so they finish on Day 3 for instant points

### Day 4 — Resource Gathering (or Beast Slay in early Gen 1)

- Send all marches to high-tier resource tiles
- Use Gathering Speed buffs to maximize per-march yield
- Each gathered resource adds small points but compounds over hundreds of millions gathered
- If your state's HoC features Beast Slay (early Gen 1 only): use all Stamina Cans on the highest-level beasts you can solo

### Day 5 — Power Boost (Hero Gear) ⭐

**Often the highest-point day. Plan accordingly.**

- Use Hero Gear Essence Stones (Mastery Forging)
- Use Hero Gear Widgets (Exclusive Gear unlock/upgrade)
- Standard Day 1 activities (construction, research) continue to score
- Veterans save substantial Essence Stone stockpiles specifically for Day 5

### Day 6 — Chief Gear Upgrade

- Upgrade Chief Gear to score Chief Gear Score points
- Use Polishing Solution, Hardened Alloy, Design Plans
- Continue Chief Charm upgrades (still scoring)

### Day 7 — Hero Development (Final Sprint)

- **Burn remaining stockpile.** This is the day to unload everything you held back.
- Final Lucky Wheel spins (90k points each — major ranking push)
- Hero ascensions with any remaining shards
- All saved speedups
- This day decides Total Ranking — players close to top 100 push hardest here

### Post-Event

- Stage Ranking and Total Ranking rewards distribute via mail
- Milestone rewards distribute throughout the event as you cross thresholds
- ~2 weeks until next HoC

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Newer Player

HoC is one of the most F2P-friendly pre-SvS events because it teaches the daily-theme discipline you'll need for KoI and SvS later. Three priorities:

1. **Hit Tier 4 milestone every day.** This is the main reward stream available without spending.
2. **Stockpile religiously between events.** The 2-week gap is enough time to build serious resource depth if you don't spend it on routine play.
3. **Don't chase top 10.** Hero of the Season shards (Jeronimo / Philly) at top 10 are typically pay-gated. Aim for top 100 stage rankings (achievable) and milestone rewards (consistent).

### Mid-Tier Player

- Same fundamentals plus: more aggressive Day 5 and Day 7 pushes
- Top 30 stage rankings achievable with good preparation
- Pre-queue large builds and big training batches the day before their stage for instant scoring

### Heavy Spender

- Top 10 Total Ranking is the realistic target for Jeronimo or Philly shards
- Pack purchases for Hero Shards, Lucky Chips, and Speedups multiply your point output significantly
- Day 5 and Day 7 are where spending compounds most — Hero Gear materials and final-sprint speedups

### R5 / Alliance Coordinator

- Remind alliance members which day is which (the daily theme rotation isn't always obvious in-game)
- Coordinate Lucky Wheel timing on Day 2 — alliance members pooling spins for milestone days helps everyone
- Track who's behind on Tier 4 milestones and offer resource help where possible

## Common Mistakes

1. **Using Hero Shards on Day 1.** They score zero. Save for Day 2 (Hero Development) and Day 7 (Final Hero Development).

2. **Spending Hero Gear Essence Stones during Day 1's Power Boost.** Day 5 is the proper day — same items, dramatically more points.

3. **Skipping Day 4 (Gathering) because "it's just gathering."** Small per-action points compound. Send all marches to high-tier tiles, use Gathering Speed buffs.

4. **Pre-queuing builds incorrectly.** Builds that complete **during** a Power Boost stage score points. Builds that complete before the stage starts score nothing. Time your build start times so they finish AFTER the stage starts.

5. **Not promoting troops on Day 3.** Promotion gives more points per troop than fresh training of the same tier. Save your lower-tier troop stockpile specifically for promotion on Train Troops day.

6. **Trying to top 10 as F2P.** The Hero of the Season top-10 reward is effectively pay-gated. Don't burn extra resources chasing it; aim for top 100 instead.

7. **Forgetting that gear materials don't carry value-equivalence between days.** Chief Gear materials on Day 6 score points; the same materials on Day 5 score very little.

8. **Not Lucky Wheel spinning on Day 7.** Day 7 is the final Hero Development stage. Lucky Wheel spins worth ~90k points each are the highest single-action ranking push available. Save Lucky Chips for it.

9. **Treating all Lucky Wheel spins as equal.** The wheel scores most heavily during Hero Development stages (Day 2 and Day 7). Spinning on other days gives normal rewards but no/few HoC points.

10. **Burning resources early.** Day 1 looks tempting because it's the first scoring opportunity, but Days 5 and 7 typically yield 2-3× more points per resource for the same items. Spread your stockpile, weighted toward the back half.

## Where Hall of Chiefs Goes Next

After your state's first State of Power event, Hall of Chiefs is **permanently replaced by [King of Icefield](/events/king-of-icefield)**. KoI is essentially HoC with:

- 7-day standard duration (matches Gen 2 HoC)
- Cross-state competition (6 states matched, not just your own)
- Significantly upgraded rewards (Mythic Hero shards of current generation, Charm Designs, Chief Gear materials)
- Same daily theme structure (the muscle memory transfers)

The skills you build in HoC — daily-theme discipline, stockpiling rhythm, day-by-day resource matching, milestone-first priority — apply directly to KoI and to SvS Preparation Phase. Hall of Chiefs is the training ground for the rest of the game's competitive event calendar.
