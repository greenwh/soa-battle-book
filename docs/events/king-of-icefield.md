# King of Icefield

::: tip
**Quick take:** Seven-day cross-state scoring event running Monday through Sunday. Replaces Hall of Chiefs after your state's first SvS. Each day has a theme — different items score on different days. Cross-state rankings against six matched states. Day 5 (Hero Gear / Mithril) is the highest-value day. Less competitive than SvS but with better baseline rewards than Hall of Chiefs. KoI and SvS alternate roughly every 2 weeks in your state's cycle.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Approximately monthly, alternating with SvS in your state's 4-week cycle (some states see KoI every 2 weeks if SvS matchmaking fails)
- **Duration:** 7 days, Monday 00:00 UTC → Sunday 23:59 UTC
- **Eligibility:** Your state must have completed at least one SvS event. Once unlocked, KoI replaces Hall of Chiefs permanently. All players in the state can participate; no power-tier restrictions.
- **Cross-state matchup:** 6 states matched together for cross-state leaderboards
- **Why it matters:** Mythic Hero shards (current generation's featured hero), Mythic Skill Manuals, Charm Designs, Charm Guides, Essence Stones, Fire Crystals, Chief Designs, Daybreak Island Construction Contracts, large gem allotments. Cross-state rankings unlock additional rewards.

## How It Works

KoI is a **resource-spending competition**. You don't fight other players — you score points by completing daily themed tasks (upgrading buildings, training troops, spinning Lucky Wheel, using Mithril, etc.). Each day has a different theme; using the right resources on the right day maximizes points.

The event has **five parallel reward streams**:

1. **Daily Milestone Rewards** — 4 tiers of point thresholds per day, with rewards at each tier
2. **Medal of Honor Rewards** — earn 2 Medals per day (14 total) by hitting Tier 3 and Tier 4 milestones; spend in the Medal Shop
3. **Stage Ranking** — top 100 in your state per day, by that day's points
4. **Total Ranking** — top 100 in your state at event end, by total cumulative points
5. **Cross-State Personal Ranking + State Ranking** — across the 6 matched states; top 1,000 personal and 250,000+ state-rank thresholds

::: warning Critical: secure milestones first, ranking second
The single most important strategic insight from competitive players: **clear Tier 4 on as many days as possible before chasing rankings.** Tier 4 unlocks the second Medal each day. Missing a day's medals removes 14% of your medal budget — and medals can't be earned retroactively. Once Tier 4 is secured, additional points only contribute to ranking, which has its own diminishing-returns calculation.
:::

### KoI vs Hall of Chiefs

If your state hasn't done SvS yet, you're playing Hall of Chiefs (covered in its own guide). Once your state completes its first SvS, Hall of Chiefs **permanently** disappears and KoI takes its place. Key differences:

| Feature | Hall of Chiefs | King of Icefield |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | In-state only | Cross-state (6 states matched) |
| Reward quality | Baseline | Significantly upgraded — Mythic shards, Fire Crystals, Charm materials |
| Ranking competition | State-only top 100 | State + cross-state personal + state rank rewards |
| Strategic complexity | Lower | Higher (more reward streams to optimize) |

### KoI vs SvS Prep

KoI and SvS Prep share the **same daily theme structure** — both have 7 days of themed scoring. This isn't accidental: KoI is essentially the practice run for SvS prep. The skills, stockpile habits, and day-by-day discipline transfer directly.

Key difference: **SvS adds the Battle Phase on Saturday**. KoI doesn't. KoI ends Sunday with rankings; SvS ends Saturday with Castle Battle.

## The 7 Daily Themes

| Day | Theme | Key scoring activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1** (Mon) | **City Construction** | Building upgrades, Construction Speedups, Fire Crystals, Refined Fire Crystals, Chief Charm upgrades |
| **Day 2** (Tue) | **Hero Development** | Hero ascensions, Lucky Wheel spins, hero gear upgrades, Mithril, Hero Shards |
| **Day 3** (Wed) | **Basic Skills Up** | Pet advancement, Wild Marks, Pet refinement, Hero Shards, Chief Charm upgrades |
| **Day 4** (Thu) | **Combat Training** | Train troops, promote troops, Training Speedups |
| **Day 5** (Fri) | **Basic Skills Up** ⭐ | **Hero Gear (Essence Stones), Mithril, Charm upgrades — usually highest-point day** |
| **Day 6** (Sat) | **Combat Training** | Train/promote troops, Training Speedups, sometimes Lucky Wheel |
| **Day 7** (Sun) | **Hero Development** (final sprint) | Mixed — gathering, troop training, leftover speedups, final point pushes |

::: warning "Basic Skills Up" appears twice — they're different
Day 3 and Day 5 are both called "Basic Skills Up" in-game, but they score different activities. **Day 3** is pets + Wild Marks. **Day 5** is Hero Gear + Mithril. Same goes for Day 2 and Day 7 ("Hero Development") — slightly different activities. Always check the actual scoring tasks each day, don't rely on the day name.
:::

## Scoring & Reward Streams

### Daily Milestone Rewards (Tiers 1–4)

Each day has 4 point thresholds. Tier 4 is the day's highest threshold; hitting it requires meaningful resource investment. **Rewards include 2 Medals of Honor — one each at Tier 3 and Tier 4.** Plus speedups, resources, gems, and category-specific items (Charm Designs on Charm days, Essence Stones on hero gear days, etc.).

::: tip Server-level unlock gates
Newer servers face structural ceilings on certain Tier 4 thresholds. **Refined Fire Crystals require Furnace 6/7/8 server unlocks**, **T10/T11 troops require server progression**, **Crystal Dust requires the War Academy unlock**, and **Legendary Chief Gear becomes available around state day 180**. If your server hasn't hit those unlocks yet, certain Tier 4 milestones are mathematically harder to reach. Don't beat yourself up — the milestone thresholds scale with your Furnace level, but the structural ceiling is what it is.
:::

### Medal of Honor Rewards

You can earn up to 14 Medals across the 7 days (2 per day, requires Tier 3 + Tier 4). Medals exchange in the Medal Shop for:

- **Mythic Hero Shards** of the current season's featured hero (the headline reward — this is the primary reason serious players engage)
- **Mythic Skill Manuals**
- **Speedups**
- **Daybreak Island Construction Contract** (General Accessory — for Island progression)
- **Resources**

The featured hero rotates with each generation. If your state is in Generation 3, current featured heroes are Generation 3 heroes (Greg, Mia, Ahmose, etc.). If you're playing for a specific hero, KoI is one of the few non-paid sources of those shards.

### Stage Ranking (Daily, top 100 in-state)

Top 100 in your state per day, by that day's points. Pays out daily.

### Total Ranking (Event end, top 100 in-state)

Top 100 in your state at event end, by cumulative points across all 7 days. **Total Ranking bundles are typically larger than Stage Ranking** — chasing total ranking is generally better value than chasing one specific day's stage ranking, but requires consistency across all 7 days.

### Cross-State Personal Ranking

Top 1,000 chiefs across the 6 matched states. Combined leaderboard. The exact point floor varies by state generation and isn't reliably documented in advance, but on most servers, top 1,000 cross-state requires solid Tier 4 clears across most days plus some serious resource expenditure on the highest-value days.

### State Rank Rewards

Awarded to chiefs scoring **250,000+ total points**. Threshold-based, not ranking-based. Most active mid-tier players hit this comfortably with consistent daily participation.

## Phase by Phase

### Pre-Event (the week before)

**Stockpile.** This is the most important week of KoI prep. Save:

- **Fire Crystals + Refined Fire Crystals** (Day 1)
- **Mithril** (Day 2 and especially Day 5)
- **Hero Shards** (Day 2)
- **Lucky Wheel chips / Hero Recruitment items** (Day 2 and Day 6)
- **Pet refinement items + Wild Marks** (Day 3)
- **Training Speedups** (Day 4 and Day 6)
- **Hero Gear Essence Stones** (Day 5)
- **Chief Charm upgrade materials** (Days 1, 3, 5)
- **Construction + Research Speedups** (Day 1)

**Don't use these items in normal play during the prep week.** Save them for KoI scoring.

::: warning Gem-purchased speedups don't score
This is the single most common error among intermediate players: only **standard speedup items** generate KoI points. Gem-purchased speedups don't count. Make sure you're using items from your inventory, not gem-converted ones.
:::

### Day 1 (Monday) — City Construction

- Use Fire Crystals on building upgrades and research
- Use Refined Fire Crystals (30,000 points each — the single highest-value scoring item this day)
- Burn Construction and Research Speedups
- Upgrade Chief Charm levels

### Day 2 (Tuesday) — Hero Development

- Spin Lucky Wheel (8,000 points per spin — high-value)
- Ascend heroes using shards
- Use Hero Gear materials
- Use Mithril if you didn't save all of it for Day 5

### Day 3 (Wednesday) — Basic Skills Up (Pets)

- Advance pets using shards/Wild Marks
- Refine pets with Wild Marks (Common and Advanced)
- Use Pet Skill Manuals
- Continue Chief Charm upgrades

### Day 4 (Thursday) — Combat Training

- Train and promote troops
- Use all Training Speedups
- T10 and T11 promotion gives more points than fresh training of the same tier

### Day 5 (Friday) — Hero Gear / Mithril ⭐

**This is typically the highest-point day of the entire event.** Plan accordingly.

- Use Mithril (massive points)
- Use Hero Gear Essence Stones (Hero Gear Mastery Forging)
- Use Exclusive Gear Widgets if you have them
- Final Chief Charm upgrades

Veteran players save substantial Mithril stockpiles specifically for Day 5. If you've been saving Mithril for SvS instead, you might split the difference: use a portion on KoI Day 5 to secure Tier 4, save the rest for SvS.

### Day 6 (Saturday) — Combat Training (with Lucky Wheel boost)

- Train/promote troops
- Lucky Wheel spins (often boosted scoring this day)
- Use any remaining Training Speedups

### Day 7 (Sunday) — Final Sprint

- Mixed scoring — gathering, training, speedup usage
- This is the day many players unload remaining resources for a final ranking push
- **If you're close to a ranking threshold (top 100 state, top 1,000 cross-state), Day 7 is when the rankings are decided**

### Post-Event

- Stage and Total Ranking rewards distribute via mail
- Cross-State Ranking and State Rank rewards distribute via mail
- Medal Shop closes — spend any unredeemed Medals before they expire

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Minimal Spend

KoI is one of the most F2P-friendly competitive events because it rewards consistency over spending power. Three priorities:

1. **Hit Tier 4 on every day you can.** Earning all 14 Medals is the most important goal. Skipping a day costs you 2 Medals you can't recover.
2. **Stockpile religiously between events.** The 4-week gap between KoI events is enough time to build serious resource depth if you don't spend it on routine play.
3. **Don't chase rankings unless you're already at Tier 4 every day.** Ranking pushes burn extra resources for diminishing rewards. Medals first, ranking second.

The featured hero shards in the Medal Shop are the headline F2P reward. Across 6-12 months of consistent participation, you can build meaningful stars on the seasonal featured hero through medals alone.

### Light Spender

- Optional Medal Shop purchases (gem-bought medals) for additional shards
- Resource packs to enable Tier 4 on borderline days
- Don't buy ranking pushes — the ROI is poor compared to medal-shop value

### Heavy Spender

- Cross-State Personal Ranking top placements have meaningful rewards
- Multi-day resource bundles to enable consistent Tier 4 + ranking pushes
- The "first cycle of a new generation" KoI is typically the most competitive — high spenders push hardest in those events for the new featured hero

### R5 / Alliance Coordinator

- Remind alliance members to stockpile during the prep week
- Coordinate Lucky Wheel timing on Day 2 (alliances sometimes pool spins for milestone days)
- Track who's behind on Tier 4 and offer resource help where possible

## Common Mistakes

1. **Using gem-purchased speedups thinking they'll score.** They don't. Only inventory speedup items generate points.

2. **Spending Mithril on Day 1 because "you have it."** Mithril scoring is on Day 2 (Hero Gear) and Day 5 (Hero Gear). Used on Day 1, it scores zero. Match items to their scoring day.

3. **Skipping the medal day, recovering with rankings.** Missing 2 Medals on a single day is a permanent loss. You can't make up missed Medals later in the same event.

4. **Treating Day 3 (pets) and Day 5 (hero gear) the same because both are "Basic Skills Up".** They score different activities. Day 3 is pets/Wild Marks; Day 5 is hero gear/Mithril.

5. **Chasing Stage Ranking when Total Ranking is better value.** Stage Ranking pays out daily for top 100 in-state. Total Ranking pays out at event end and the bundles are typically larger. Optimizing for Total Ranking (consistent Tier 4 across all 7 days) is usually better than maximizing one day for Stage Ranking.

6. **Overspending on the first KoI cycle of a new generation.** That's when whales push hardest for the new featured hero. F2P/light spenders ranking high on cycle 1 of a new gen is mathematically very expensive. Better strategy: aim for Medal Shop purchases on cycle 1, push for ranking on cycles 2-3 when the field has cooled.

7. **Forgetting Day 5 is the highest-value day.** Plan stockpile around it. Mithril and Hero Gear materials there are worth significantly more than the same items used on lower-scoring days.

8. **Not using all 14 Medals before the shop closes.** The Medal Shop has a window. Unspent Medals expire. Plan your Medal Shop purchases before Day 7.

9. **F2P players who skip KoI because "I can't rank."** You don't need to rank. The Medal Shop is the actual reward stream. Hit Tier 4 daily and you'll come away with substantial rewards regardless of leaderboard position.

10. **Letting "I'll save everything for SvS" become "I scored badly in KoI."** SvS is more rewarding overall, but KoI is real points and real rewards. A balanced approach — hit KoI Tier 4 with the resources you have, save the bulk of Mithril/Refined FC for SvS — usually yields better total returns than going all-in on either.
