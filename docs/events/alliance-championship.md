# Alliance Championship

::: tip
**Quick take:** Weekly cross-server tournament. Six alliances of similar tier matched together for five rounds of automated three-lane battles. No active play required during combat — battles resolve automatically. **Zero troop losses.** Coordination matters in registration and lane assignment, not during fights. The most accessible competitive alliance event in the game — every member contributes just by registering with optimized troops.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Weekly (Monday 00:00 UTC start, runs Monday-to-Monday)
- **Duration:** 7-day cycle with 5 battle rounds
- **Eligibility:** Furnace Lv. 10+, "middle-ranking" or higher in the alliance (the precise definition isn't specified in-game)
- **Alliance requirement:** 10+ members must register during the Sign-Up Phase for the alliance to enter matchmaking
- **Why it matters:** Championship Badges (for the AC Shop), Hero Shards, Speedups, Resources, exclusive skins from Prestige Rewards. Long-term tier progression with Star/Tier system. **No troop losses** make it one of the highest-ROI events for active alliances.

## How It Works

Alliance Championship is fundamentally different from most other events in one key way: **the battles are fully automated.** You don't need to be online during combat. You don't send rallies. You don't reinforce. Once your troops are registered and assigned to a lane, the game runs the battle and reports results.

What matters is **what happens before** combat — registration, troop composition, hero loadout, lane assignment, and buff timing.

### The Six Phases

The full weekly cycle:

| Phase | Time (UTC) | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Sign-Up** | Monday 00:00 → Wednesday 23:00 | Alliance registers; players individually register marches |
| **Matchmaking** | Wednesday 23:00 → Thursday | System pairs your alliance with 5 others of similar tier |
| **Preparation 1** | ~11 hours before each Battle | Lane reassignment window; final adjustments |
| **Battle Rounds 1–5** | Throughout the week | 1-hour automated battles; one round per opponent |
| **Tally** | After Round 5 | Final group ranking determined |
| **Completion / Rewards** | After Tally | Tier rewards, Championship Badges distributed; AC Shop opens |

Five battle rounds because each alliance fights the other 5 alliances in the group exactly once (round-robin format).

### The Battlefield

Each match has **3 lanes** (left, middle, right), each with a flag.

- Each lane needs at least **20 main combatants** + substitutes from your alliance
- Players are placed in lanes during the Sign-Up Phase (initial placement) or by R4/R5 during Preparation Phases
- Battles happen lane-by-lane, automated
- **Win 2 of 3 lanes = win the match**
- **No troop losses** — even defeated marches return home intact

::: warning Only the top 20 marches in each lane fight
If your alliance has 25 players in the middle lane, only the **top 20 by march power** actually compete. The other 5 are substitutes and don't earn battle rewards. R5/R4 should track who's actually fighting.
:::

### Lane Combat Order

Within a single lane, marches fight in **ascending power order** — weakest goes first, strongest goes last. A victorious player who wins **two consecutive matchups** can return to fight again in that lane.

**Strategic implication:** the weakest player in your lane fights first against the enemy's weakest. If you have a power gap (your weakest is much stronger than their weakest), you'll dominate the early matchups. Conversely, if your weakest is much weaker, you'll lose the opening battles. **Stacking your strongest players in one lane is a gamble** — you'll likely win that lane, but the other two lanes face the enemy's strongest.

### Tier & Star Progression

All alliances start at the bottom tier. Final group ranking earns Stars; accumulated Stars promote your alliance to higher tiers:

| Tier (lowest to highest) | Reward Quality |
|---|---|
| Honored Tier | Baseline rewards |
| Heroic Tier | Improved rewards |
| Royal Tier | Significantly better rewards |
| Imperial Tier | Premium rewards |
| **Ultimate Tier** | **Best rewards + Prestige skins** |

Reaching Ultimate Tier unlocks **Prestige Rewards** — exclusive skins distributed to all members who participated. Tier progression is one of the core long-term goals for competitive alliances.

### Buff Capture (Community-Reported Mechanic)

This is widely reported but not officially documented: **buffs active at the moment of registration appear to be locked into your registered march.** That means activating combat buffs (pet skills, gem-purchased temp buffs, alliance buffs) **right before you register** carries those buffs into all 5 battles.

::: warning Treat this as a working assumption, not a guaranteed mechanic
The buff-capture behavior isn't confirmed in any official source. The community consensus is strong, but if you're playing optimally, register with all relevant buffs active. If buff-capture doesn't work as advertised, you've lost nothing. If it does work, you've gained 5 battles of buffed performance.
:::

## Registration & Setup

### Pre-Registration Checklist

Before you click Register:

1. **Confirm alliance has 10+ members planning to register** (the threshold for matchmaking eligibility)
2. **Optimize troop composition** — manually set to 50/20/30 (Inf/Lan/Mar). The default 33/33/33 is significantly worse.
3. **Set hero loadout** — choose your strongest 3-hero combat loadout. Expedition skills matter most.
4. **Activate buffs**:
 - Pet skills with combat effects (Polar Bear: Lethality, Musk Ox: Attack)
 - Any temporary attack/defense/HP buffs you have
 - Alliance buffs if available
 - Skin bonuses (passive, but verify they're active)
5. **Click Register** — locks in your march for the week

::: tip 50/20/30 is the standard meta
The game defaults to 33/33/33 when you click Register. **Manually adjust to 50/20/30** (Infantry/Lancer/Marksman). The infantry frontline tanks for the lancer/marksman damage in the back. This composition significantly outperforms the default in 1v1 lane combat.
:::

### Lane Assignment Strategy

A common alliance strategy:

- **All members register into the middle lane initially** during Sign-Up
- **R4/R5 redistribute players** across all three lanes after Matchmaking, based on the opponent's apparent strength

This works because:
- Initial registration just needs to happen (members aren't always online when needed)
- Final placement is the strategic decision, made when you can see the opposing alliance's roster
- Centralizing initial registration prevents "we have 30 in left, 5 in right" disasters

### What If A Player Switches Alliances Mid-Cycle?

A player who registers and then switches alliances mid-cycle has a complicated outcome:

- **Their march keeps fighting for the original alliance** for the entire event cycle
- They receive **Completion Phase tier rewards from their new alliance**
- They **forfeit KO Rewards** (knockout battle rewards) from the original alliance
- Effectively, they help their old alliance and earn from their new one — but lose some KO-specific bonuses

**Don't switch alliances mid-cycle if you can help it.**

## Phase by Phase

### Sign-Up Phase (Monday 00:00 → Wednesday 23:00 UTC)

**Goals:**

- Get 10+ members registered to qualify for matchmaking
- Each registering player optimizes their march composition (50/20/30) and hero loadout
- Activate buffs before clicking Register (community-reported buff capture)
- Default to placing all members in the middle lane unless R4/R5 has a different plan

**Common mistake:** Players register on Monday morning with default 33/33/33 composition, no hero adjustment, no buffs. Their march is locked in for the week with suboptimal stats.

### Matchmaking Phase (Wednesday 23:00 → Thursday)

**Goals:**

- System automatically matches your alliance with 5 others of similar tier
- R4/R5 reviews opponent rosters when matchmaking completes
- Plan lane redistributions based on opponent power profiles

### Preparation Phase 1 (~11 hours before Round 1)

**Goals:**

- R4/R5 reassigns players to lanes based on Round 1's opponent
- Confirm everyone is still registered (no surprise leavers)
- Verify lane counts (each lane needs at least 20 to compete properly)

### Battle Rounds 1–5

**Battles are 1 hour each, automated.** Players don't need to be online. Members just check results afterward.

Between rounds:

- **Preparation Phase ~11 hours before each subsequent round**
- R4/R5 reviews previous round results, identifies weak lanes
- Reassigns players to optimize against the next opponent's profile
- A march can fight at most **twice per round** (this caps lane reassignment churn)

### Tally Phase

After Round 5:

- Final group rankings determined by total wins (1st through 6th place)
- Stars distributed based on placement
- Tier promotion or demotion determined

### Completion Phase

- Tier rewards distribute
- Championship Badges distribute
- Prestige Rewards (Ultimate Tier only) distribute
- AC Shop opens — Championship Badges can be exchanged for Hero Shards, Speedups, exclusive items
- Shop refreshes every 7 days; stock is randomized per player (you may see different items than alliance mates)

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Newer Player

Alliance Championship is the **most accessible competitive event** for F2P:

1. **Register every Monday.** Showing up = rewards.
2. **Manually set 50/20/30 troop composition.** This single change vs. the default is the biggest free improvement available.
3. **Optimize hero loadout for combat.** Use your strongest 3 heroes with combat-relevant Expedition skills.
4. **Activate any buffs you have before clicking Register.** Pet skills (Polar Bear, Musk Ox), small attack/defense buffs, alliance buffs.
5. **Don't switch alliances mid-cycle.**

You contribute to your alliance's tier progression with zero risk of troop loss. Even an outclassed alliance member who optimizes their setup adds meaningful pressure to the opposing alliance's lanes.

### Mid-Tier Player

- Same fundamentals plus: invest in better hero gear specifically for AC competitive value
- Volunteer for the strongest lane your alliance is contesting
- Help with R4/R5 lane reassignment by showing up reliably during Preparation Phases

### Whale / Top Player

- Your power decides whether your alliance can climb to Ultimate Tier
- Lead the strongest lane in each round
- Coordinate with R5 on lane assignments — your placement is a strategic decision, not a default
- Maximize hero gear, Chief Gear, and Mythic skill manuals — they directly impact AC win rates

### R5 / Alliance Coordinator

- **Register your alliance Monday morning.** Don't wait — late registration risks missing the matchmaking window.
- **Build a "who registered with what loadout" tracker.** Members who register with bad compositions reduce your match win rate every battle.
- **Lane reassignment is your highest-leverage activity.** Review opponent rosters in each Preparation Phase and adjust.
- **Communicate buff timing.** If your alliance has temporary buffs (Bear Trap, etc.), schedule them to be active during AC registration windows.
- **Accept the long game.** Tier promotion happens over months, not weeks.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Leaving troop composition at 33/33/33.** The default is significantly worse than 50/20/30. This is the single most-cited mistake across all sources.

2. **Registering without activating buffs.** If buff capture works (widely reported), buffs at registration carry into all 5 battles. Don't waste this.

3. **Stacking strongest players in one lane.** You'll dominate that lane but face their strongest in the other two. Spread your strength to win 2 of 3 lanes consistently.

4. **Switching alliances mid-cycle.** You forfeit KO Rewards from the original alliance and your march continues fighting for them anyway. Wait until after the cycle ends.

5. **Treating it as a passive event.** It IS automated, but the prep work (composition, heroes, buffs, lane assignment) determines outcomes. Players who think "register and forget" miss most of the value.

6. **Not using all your Championship Badges before the shop refreshes.** Shop refreshes every 7 days; unspent badges might be wasted if items disappear. Spend regularly.

7. **Missing the registration window entirely.** Sign-Up closes Wednesday 23:00 UTC. Late registrations don't qualify.

8. **R5/R4 forgetting to redistribute lanes after Matchmaking.** If everyone registered into the middle lane and stays there, your left and right lanes auto-forfeit. Spread members out before Round 1.

9. **Using growth heroes (Zinman, Charlie) in your hero loadout.** Combat heroes only — first skill matters for direct combat damage, not hero growth bonuses.

10. **Substitute players assuming they'll get rewards.** Only the top 20 marches by power in each lane actually compete. Substitutes don't earn battle rewards. R5 should manage substitute communication.

## Tier Progression Long Game

Alliance Championship rewards consistency. A few months of steady Top-3 finishes climbs you from Honored to Heroic to Royal tier. Each promotion improves baseline rewards.

Reaching **Ultimate Tier** is the long-term goal — it unlocks Prestige Rewards (exclusive skins) for every member who participated. Most competitive alliances target this within 6-12 months of dedicated AC participation.

If your alliance is bouncing between tiers (promote one cycle, demote the next), the issue is usually **inconsistent registration** — not power gap. Get every member to register every Monday with optimized loadouts, and the win rate stabilizes.
