# Fishing Tournament

::: tip
**Quick take:** Monthly 3-day mini-game with seasonal fish rotations. No combat, no risk — just a casual side activity that drops permanent stat-boost skins for your furnace, march transport, and avatar. The +2% Troop Attack and +2% Troop Defense skins from collection completion are some of the best free permanent buffs in the game.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Duration:** 3 days (typically Tuesday 00:00 → Thursday 23:59)
- **Eligibility:** All players — no Furnace level requirement, no Alliance requirement
- **Why it matters:** Permanent skin rewards with stat boosts (+2% Troop Attack, +5% Solo March Speed, +2% Troop Defense), Mythic Hero Shards from the Sunken Treasure Hunt, gems, speedups, and Essence Stones
- **Seasonal:** Rotates through Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter; full collection requires participating across multiple seasons

## How It Works

A casual mini-game played at the Fishing Hut in your city. There's no combat, no troop loss, and no Alliance dependency. The core loop:

1. Drop your hook into the water
2. Avoid obstacles on the way down (fish, debris)
3. When the hook stops, swipe to catch fish on the way back up
4. Earn Fishing Tokens (used for upgrades and the Sunken Treasure Hunt) and Fishing Points (used for leaderboard ranking and Ice Fishing Club rewards)

Two fishing modes are available:

### Ice Fishing — the default mode

- Costs 1 Bait per attempt
- You start the event with 5 Baits and regenerate 1 Bait every 3 hours, capped at 10
- Your equipment levels (Line, Hook, Sinker) determine your performance — you upgrade them with Fishing Tokens earned in this mode
- Less rare fish, but it's how you earn the tokens to upgrade your gear and trigger Sunken Treasure Hunt spins

### Frosty Prospector — the premium mode

- Costs 1 Treasure Chart per attempt
- All equipment is automatically set to max level (550m line depth, 20-fish hook capacity, 165m sinker depth)
- Significantly higher rare-fish rates and access to exclusive sunken chests
- Treasure Charts come from the Fishing Pro Set (paid pack), Ice Fishing Club milestones, or daily mission rewards

::: warning Don't let your bait cap
Bait regenerates while you're not playing — but if you hit the 10-bait maximum, the regen stops. Check in at least every 30 hours to spend down your bait, or you waste regeneration time.
:::

## Equipment

You upgrade three pieces during Ice Fishing using Fishing Tokens. Max level on each is 10.

| Piece | Effect at Lv.10 | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Line** | Reaches 550m depth | Determines how deep you can fish — rare fish live deep |
| **Hook** | Catches up to 20 fish per cast | Determines how many fish you can grab on the way up |
| **Sinker** | Starts at 165m depth | Lets you skip shallow obstacles entirely |

In Frosty Prospector, all three are automatically maxed.

### Upgrade priority

This is where community guides disagree. Two competing strategies:

**Strategy A — Hook first.** Catching more fish per cast directly multiplies your point output for every remaining cast in the event. Hook 1 catches 5 fish; Hook 10 catches 20 fish. That's a 4× output multiplier on the same dive.

**Strategy B — Line first.** Deeper water has rarer (higher-point) fish. A 5-star fish at 500m can be worth more than four 3-star fish at 200m. Reaching the depth where rare fish spawn unlocks higher per-cast scores.

**The practical answer:** Both upgrades pay off. Most data-driven guides lean toward **Hook first** because the point multiplier is more reliable than depending on RNG-rare fish appearing in a specific cast. But Line is a close second and Sinker is clearly third.

The simple rule: **alternate Hook and Line upgrades, finish Sinker last.** Pure Hook-first costs you depth access for too long; pure Line-first costs you catch volume.

## Special Items

These appear as drops or as Fishing Pro Set / Ice Fishing Club rewards. They're worth saving for big runs.

| Item | Effect | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Treasure Chart** | Required for Frosty Prospector mode | Save until your Ice Fishing gear is at Lv.10, then dump them |
| **Horn of Poseidon** | Summons one specific fish for guaranteed catch | Use on highest-rarity fish you haven't caught yet |
| **Icefisher Voucher** | Multiplies fishing score by 1.5× for one round | Combine with Frosty Prospector + Horn of Poseidon for max-point runs |
| **Reel Stabilizer** | Prevents your line from retracting on +2 obstacle hits | Useful in heavy-debris areas |
| **Ocean Scanner** | Reveals positions of nearby rare fish | Worth using in Frosty Prospector |
| **Lantern** | Illuminates depths beyond 300m | Pair with high-Line builds for visibility |

::: tip The max-point combo
Treasure Chart + Horn of Poseidon + Icefisher Voucher in a single Frosty Prospector run guarantees one 5-star fish, multiplies the entire score by 1.5×, and accesses the deepest fish. This is the highest-output single run available — save your tools for it.
:::

## The Collection System (Guides)

There are four collection guides; completing each unlocks a permanent reward.

| Guide | Target | Reward | Permanent Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fish Guide** | 150 unique fish | Treasure Stronghold (Furnace skin) | **+2% Troop Attack** |
| **Diver Guide** | 10 unique divers | Ice Journey (March skin) | **+5% Solo Hunting March Speed** |
| **Chest Guide** | 10 unique chests | Romance of Sailors (Nameplate) | None (cosmetic) |
| **Memorabilia Guide** | 60 unique memorabilia items | Romance of Sailors (Avatar Frame) | **+2% Troop Defense** |

Three of the four reward permanent stat boosts. **The Fish Guide and Memorabilia Guide together give a permanent +2% Troop Attack and +2% Troop Defense** — these are among the best free permanent buffs available to any player at any progression level.

::: warning Multi-season grind
You will not finish the Fish Guide in one event. Each season has unique fish and you need to participate across multiple seasons to catch all 150. Sources estimate **3 seasons of consistent participation** is the minimum needed for the Fish Guide. Memorabilia and Diver collections also span seasons. Plan for it as a 6–12 month commitment, not a single-event grind.
:::

### Restart mechanism (the key collection trick)

You can quit the game **before** the results screen appears at the end of a Frosty Prospector run, and re-enter — fish positions reshuffle, but the fish types stay the same. For Ice Fishing, the Memorabilia and 2-star Diver assignments change. This means you can use the restart trick to repeatedly attempt to catch unfamiliar Memorabilia or Divers without burning bait.

The Retreat button (mid-dive pause) similarly resets your line **without consuming bait or Treasure Charts** — useful for practicing routes or recovering from a bad descent.

## Scoring & Rewards

Three reward streams overlap during the event:

### Master Fisher Leaderboard

Your total Fishing Points across the 3 days is ranked across all participants on your server.

- **Top 10:** Premium gems, large speedup bundles, exclusive cosmetics
- **Top 100:** Significant gem rewards, training speedups
- **Top 500:** Solid gem and resource rewards
- **Outside Top 500:** Smaller participation rewards

The leaderboard is the most competitive reward stream. Active F2P players targeting Top 500 with consistent play. Top 100 typically requires the Fishing Pro pack or heavy use of Treasure Charts saved across events.

### Ice Fishing Club (point milestones)

Hit Fishing Point thresholds (250, 500, 1,000, 1,500, 2,000+) for tiered rewards regardless of leaderboard position. Free track includes Treasure Charts, Horn of Poseidon, Mythic Hero Shards, and Essence Stones. The paid Fishing Pro and Fishing Master tracks add stacking +10%/+20% Fishing Tokens and Points multipliers.

### Sunken Treasure Hunt (after maxing gear)

Once all three pieces of equipment are at Lv.10, additional Fishing Points convert to spins on the Sunken Treasure Hunt — a lucky-wheel mechanic costing 1,500 points per spin. Possible rewards include:

- Mythic General Hero Shards (multiple per spin possible)
- Mythic Expedition Skill Manuals
- Mythic Exploration Manuals
- Essence Stones
- General Speedups
- Resource bundles

The Sunken Treasure Hunt is where most of the late-event points go for active players. Once your gear is maxed, every additional Fishing Token converts to a chance at Mythic shards or skill manuals.

## Phase by Phase

### Day 1 (Tuesday)

**Open the event and run the daily missions.** Each day brings a fresh set of point-earning missions that reset on server reset.

**Burn your starting 5 baits in Ice Fishing.** Goal: earn enough Fishing Tokens to upgrade Hook to Lv.3 or Lv.4 and Line to Lv.2 or Lv.3. Don't worry about rare fish yet — focus on catch volume to fund upgrades.

**Use any saved Treasure Charts on Frosty Prospector.** Frosty Prospector starts at maxed gear, so it's higher-point per attempt regardless of where your real gear is. If you've saved Treasure Charts from previous events, dump them now to fund mid-event upgrades.

### Day 2 (Wednesday)

**Continue upgrading.** Goal by end of Day 2: Hook Lv.10, Line Lv.10, Sinker at Lv.5+. This unlocks Sunken Treasure Hunt access.

**Use Horn of Poseidon strategically.** Save Horn for fish you haven't caught yet (check the Fish Guide tab). Use it on the rarest unfilled species, not on a 3-star common.

**Watch for collection items.** The Memorabilia and Chest items appear randomly during Ice Fishing. The restart mechanism lets you reroll if a run doesn't have what you need.

### Day 3 (Thursday)

**Finish Sinker upgrades** if you haven't yet (you should have started Day 3 with both Hook and Line at Lv.10).

**Burn all remaining items.** Treasure Charts, Horns of Poseidon, Icefisher Vouchers — none of these carry over with full value. Use them.

**Run the max-point combo.** A Treasure Chart + Horn of Poseidon + Icefisher Voucher run on a 5-star target species is your single highest-output cast. Time it so it counts toward both leaderboard ranking and Sunken Treasure Hunt spins.

**Spend remaining Fishing Tokens on Sunken Treasure Hunt** at 1,500 tokens per spin. Each spin is an RNG chance at Mythic Shards or Skill Manuals.

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Minimal Spend

You can complete the permanent skin collections as F2P over time. Three priorities:

1. **Save Treasure Charts across events.** Don't burn them as soon as they drop — saving 3–5 Treasure Charts to spend on Day 3 of a tournament is much higher per-charge value than spending them as you earn them.
2. **Aim for Top 500 leaderboard.** This is achievable with consistent play and no cash investment, and the gem rewards add up over multiple events.
3. **Prioritize collection completion over leaderboard ranking.** The permanent +2% Attack and +2% Defense skins are alliance-changing over your account's lifetime; a one-event leaderboard rank isn't.

The free Ice Fishing Club track delivers Treasure Charts, Horns of Poseidon, and Mythic shards — these are real F2P rewards.

### Light Spender

The Fishing Pro pack is the highest-value optional purchase: stacking +10% Fishing Tokens and Points across the entire event, plus the Fishing Pro Set bonus items (1 Treasure Chart, 1 Horn of Poseidon, 1 Icefisher Voucher, etc.).

The Fishing Master pack adds another +10% on top, plus combat buffs (Troops Attack/Defense/HP, Damage Up). For active fishers chasing Top 100, the Fishing Master pays for itself in leaderboard rewards.

### Heavy Spender

Multiple Treasure Chart purchases enable repeated Frosty Prospector runs. Top 10 leaderboard typically requires this level of investment. The cosmetic-only top rewards aren't a clear value driver — most heavy spenders treat Fishing Tournament as a casual event rather than a primary investment target.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Letting your bait cap.** 10/10 bait means regeneration has stopped. Check in at least every 30 hours to spend down to free up regen.

2. **Burning Treasure Charts early.** Frosty Prospector mode is at maxed gear regardless of your real gear. So burning Treasure Charts on Day 1 when your Ice Fishing gear is unupgraded means you're getting Frosty Prospector benefits without leveraging the gear progression. Save them for Day 2/3 when you have items stacked for the Treasure Chart + Horn + Voucher combo.

3. **Ignoring the Memorabilia/Diver collections.** Players obsess over the Fish Guide (150 fish) and forget the Memorabilia Guide (60 items, +2% Troop Defense) is similarly valuable and easier to grind because of the restart mechanism.

4. **Pure Line-first or pure Hook-first upgrade strategy.** Both extremes leave performance on the table. Alternate them.

5. **Spending Fishing Tokens on Sunken Treasure Hunt before maxing gear.** Sunken Treasure Hunt only opens at Lv.10 across all three pieces. Tokens spent on upgrades aren't wasted — they're prerequisite to the Hunt.

6. **Forgetting to use Horn of Poseidon on uncaught species.** Horn guarantees a specific fish. Wasting it on a fish you've already cataloged adds points but doesn't progress the Fish Guide collection (which is where the permanent stat-boost skin lives).

7. **Treating it as a daily event.** It's monthly. Three days, then gone for ~27 days. Plan accordingly — don't burn through bait in Day 1 and have nothing left for Day 3's max-point combo runs.

8. **Skipping the event because "it's just a mini-game."** The permanent +2% Troop Attack and +2% Troop Defense skins are among the best free buffs in Whiteout Survival. Skipping costs your account permanent stats forever.
