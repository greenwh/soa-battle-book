# Snowbusters

::: tip
**Quick take:** Monthly 3-day mini-game where you control Buster, a snowplow, through 25 stages. Manage fuel, rescue survivors, defeat beasts, collect map fragments. Permanent rewards include cosmetic skins, gems, and a unique Snowbusters island building unlocked at 20-star milestone. Lower-stakes than combat events but rewards build account value over time.
:::

## At a Glance

- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Duration:** 3 days per occurrence
- **Eligibility:** All players in States 4-170 (broader rollout has expanded since launch — confirm event appears in your event menu)
- **Theme rotation:** Four rotating seasonal themes (Spring/Summer/Autumn/Winter style); each event uses one theme
- **Why it matters:** Permanent cosmetic skins (city skins, march effects, frames, nameplates), gems, **a unique island building unlockable at 20 stars**, and Map Fragments that carry over between events for the Map Search collection grind

## How It Works

A real-time snow-clearing mini-game played through the Event menu. There's no combat with your actual troops, no risk to your city, and no Alliance dependency. You're playing as a single character (Buster) in a contained mini-game environment.

**The core loop in each stage:**

1. Spawn at the central Fire Pit (your safe zone)
2. Clear surrounding snow using your Snowbuster, consuming Fuel
3. Rescue Survivors to expand your snow-clearing range
4. Defeat Beasts and Snowmen for Coal, Map Fragments, and other drops
5. Collect Coal to upgrade your Fire Pit (which regenerates HP and reduces fuel cost)
6. Reach **95% snow clearance** to trigger an auto-clear rocket and advance to the next stage

The goal is **not** to clear 100% of every stage — that wastes fuel. It's to hit 95%, grab the high-value targets, and move on.

### Resources

| Resource | Source | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Fuel** | Starts at 200 per event, regenerates ~12 per 5 minutes; also from beasts/snowmen | Powers Buster's actions (clearing, fighting, rescuing) |
| **Coal** | Clearing snow, defeating beasts/snowmen | Upgrades the Fire Pit; converts to bonus Fuel at end of stage |
| **Snowbuster Toolkits** | Stage rewards | Upgrades your gear (resets each event) |
| **Map Fragments** | Defeating beasts | Permanent collection items — carry over between events |
| **Stars** | First-time map collection unlocks | Track toward 20-star milestone for the island building |

::: warning Fuel Supply Chests carry over, opened Fuel doesn't
Unopened **Fuel Supply Chests** in your backpack carry over between events. **Opened Fuel does not.** Don't open chests if you can't burn the fuel before the event ends — it disappears. Same rule applies to Snowbuster Toolkit Chests (unopened carry over, but the Toolkits inside do not).
:::

### Hazards

- **Snowstorms** — appear randomly, more frequently as you clear more snow. Move out of the red zone immediately or take damage.
- **Beasts in uncleared snow** — can ambush you when you reach them. Approach with full HP if possible.
- **HP at zero** — Buster gets "Severely Injured" and must return to a Fire Pit to recover. **You drop 10% of your Coal** and lose all active buffs.

## Gear

You upgrade four pieces of gear during the event using **Snowbuster Toolkits** earned from stage rewards. Gear **resets** at the end of every event — you start fresh each month.

| Gear | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Snowbuster** | Increases attack and snow-clearing range |
| **Gloves** | Increases Health and reduces Fuel consumption |
| **Coat** | Increases damage mitigation and **Fuel recovery speed** |
| **Snow Boots** | Increases movement speed and increases healing near the Fire Pit |

### Upgrade priority

This is where community guides disagree, similar to Fishing's Hook vs. Line debate.

**Strategy A — Coat first.** Coat boosts Fuel recovery speed and damage mitigation. Data-driven guides argue this is the highest leverage because Fuel is the constraint of the entire event. More fuel recovery = more attempts = more progress.

**Strategy B — Snowbuster + Gloves first.** Snowbuster increases your attack and clearing range; Gloves boost Health and lower Fuel use per action. This is the "more direct combat power" approach — useful for clearing each stage faster.

**The practical answer for most players:** **Upgrade Coat to maximum on Day 1** (the data-driven recommendation), then alternate Snowbuster and Gloves. Save Boots for last. Coat's fuel-recovery boost compounds across the entire event — every minute of accelerated regen pays dividends across multiple stages.

If you're running short on Toolkits and only progressing through ~15 stages, the alternative (Snowbuster + Gloves) gets you through the early stages faster. But **for anyone targeting Stage 16+ or higher rewards, Coat-first is the better play.**

## Special Items (Stackable Buffs)

These appear as drops during stages and are **stackable** — combining them dramatically improves performance:

| Item | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Super Snowbuster** | Expands range, +20% movement speed for 20 seconds |
| **Infini-Fuel** | Unlimited Fuel for short duration |
| **Rocket** | Auto-clears a large area without consuming Fuel |
| **Ultra Magnet** | Auto-collects nearby Coal and items |
| **Portable Snowshield** | Immunity to next 3 attacks (does NOT stack — replaces existing shield) |
| **Medkit** | Restores HP |

::: tip The "stack everything" strategy
The single biggest performance multiplier in Snowbusters is **stacking Super Snowbuster + Infini-Fuel simultaneously.** Save these buffs by walking past them without picking up duplicates, then trigger both at once when you encounter a difficult area or a large Snowman. Same logic applies to Rocket + Ultra Magnet (clear and collect simultaneously) and Portable Snowshield + Medkit (survive heavy enemy encounters).
:::

::: warning Rocket V2 carries over; regular Rockets don't
**Unopened Rocket V2 items** stay in your backpack between events. **Don't waste them on early stages.** Save them for difficult late stages (16+) or for accumulating across multiple events to push deep on a single attempt.
:::

## Stage Progression

The event has **25 stages**. Performance per stage is gated by Fuel — early stages are easy, mid-game requires gear and strategy, late stages are deliberately punishing.

### The Stage 16 cliff

A pattern most experienced Snowbusters players follow:

- **Stages 1-15** are progressively harder but manageable on natural Fuel regeneration
- **Stage 16** is significantly more rewarding than 15 and still clearable with disciplined play
- **Stages 17-18** require a major Fuel investment for marginal reward improvement
- **Stage 19** offers the best rewards but is significantly harder than 18
- **Stages 20-25** are increasingly punishing

**The standard recommendation:**

> **After clearing Stage 16, stop. Convert all remaining Fuel to Rocket items. Save your Rocket V2 items across multiple events, then use them all at once to push to Stage 19.**

This is the cost-effective progression path for most players. Stages 17-18 are "trap stages" — you'll spend too much fuel for not enough reward improvement. The leap from 18 to 19 is significant, so save up and make the jump in a single event.

### Bonus stages (5, 10, 15)

Every 5 stages is a "bonus" stage with **unlimited Fuel** for the duration. These are gifts — clear everything, defeat all beasts, open all chests. Don't rush these; the Fuel is free.

## Movement & Efficiency

A few mechanics worth knowing:

**Forward speed slows when clearing snow, turning speed doesn't.** Sweeping in arcs covers more ground than walking in straight lines. Most efficient pattern: sweep in curves around the Fire Pit, expanding outward.

**Don't clear snow near the Fire Pit.** The Fire auto-clears its own area (the radius grows with Fire Pit level). Burning Fuel inside the Fire's auto-clear zone wastes fuel.

**Walk near the Fire to reset Beast aggro.** If you've drawn multiple Beasts and need to recover, return to the Fire — Beasts disengage.

**Lure beasts toward Snowmen.** If you can position so an attack hits both, you maximize Coal and Fragment drops. Same logic for clustering Beasts near Medkits or Portable Snowshields you've spotted but not yet picked up.

## Collections & Rewards

Three reward streams overlap during the event:

### Map Fragment Collection (the long game)

Defeating Beasts drops Map Fragments tied to the **current theme**. Collecting unique fragments unlocks:

| Stars | Reward |
|---|---|
| Various milestones | Permanent cosmetic skins (city, march, frame, nameplate) — themed |
| **20 stars** | **Snowbusters Island Building** (permanent) |

The 20-star milestone for the **Snowbusters Island Building** is the headline long-term reward. This building is a permanent fixture on your Daybreak Island and **cannot be leveled with Mythic General Shards** — only with building pieces earned from this event. Multi-event grind required.

**Map Search:** Duplicate fragments automatically convert into Map Fragments (a separate currency) usable to redeem additional collection items. **Map Fragments carry over between events** — Stars and unique map collection progress carry over too.

::: warning Theme rotation matters
Map Fragments only count toward the **current theme**. If you need a Spring-themed item, you have to wait for a Spring-themed event. Plan multi-event progression accordingly.
:::

### Stage Rewards (Snowbuster Toolkits + resources)

Each stage cleared awards Snowbuster Toolkits (for gear), resources (meat, wood, coal, iron), gems, and small amounts of speedups. Higher stages yield better rewards.

### Snow Warrior Packs (Paid)

Two paid passes (~$5 and ~$10) offer extra rewards parallel to the event. Optional — most rewards are accessible without packs.

## Phase by Phase

### Day 1 (Day of event start)

**Open the event and play through the easy stages.** Stages 1-5 burn through your starting 200 Fuel quickly. Use them to:
- Earn enough Snowbuster Toolkits to upgrade Coat to max
- Start unlocking the Map Fragment collection
- Get a feel for the controls and movement mechanics

**Bonus Stage 5 is unlimited Fuel** — clear everything, open every chest, kill every beast.

By end of Day 1, target: Coat at max level, Snowbuster and Gloves at level 3-4, all 5 bonus stages cleared (5, 10, 15 if reachable).

### Day 2 (Mid-event)

**Push to Stage 16.** Use natural Fuel regeneration and any saved buffs.

**Stack Super Snowbuster + Infini-Fuel** in the harder stages (12+). The combo dramatically increases your effective Fuel.

**Don't open Fuel Supply Chests early.** Wait until you have a Fuel deficit and an opportunity to use them (e.g., before tackling Stage 14 or 15).

### Day 3 (Final push)

**Hit Stage 16 if you haven't yet.** This is the standard "good enough" target.

**The Stage 19 push.** If you've stockpiled Rocket V2 items from previous events (or earned several this event), today's the day. Burn them all to push to Stage 19. Don't waste them on Stage 17 or 18.

**Use remaining Fuel and items.** Anything left over is wasted — burn it through Stage rewards.

**Convert remaining Coal to Fuel rewards** in your final stage clear by saving Coal upgrades to the Fire Pit until they're needed for that stage's clearance.

## Strategy by Player Type

### F2P / Minimal Spend

Snowbusters is one of the most F2P-friendly events in the game — gear progression is event-locked, so spenders don't have a permanent advantage in any single event. Two priorities:

1. **Aim for Stage 16 every event.** This is the consistent, achievable target without packs.
2. **Save Rocket V2 across events.** Stockpiling 3-5 V2 Rockets enables a Stage 19 push every few events for the better rewards. The 20-star island building unlock is the multi-event grind.

The Snowbusters Island Building unlocks at 20 stars and **takes multiple events to complete**. Don't expect it from a single playthrough.

### Light Spender

The $5 Snow Warrior Pack typically pays for itself if you're playing actively — extra Toolkits accelerate gear upgrades, extra Fuel Supply Chests cover Stage 16-19 attempts.

The $10 pack adds enough Fuel and Toolkits to comfortably reach Stage 19 in a single event without saving Rocket V2 across events. Useful if you're pushing for the Snowbusters Island Building faster.

### Heavy Spender

Multiple Fuel Supply Chest packs allow attempts at Stages 20-25 in a single event. The cosmetic-only rewards at high stages aren't a clear value driver — most heavy spenders treat Snowbusters as a casual event rather than a primary investment target. The island building progression is the only real long-term goal that benefits from heavy spending.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Trying to clear 100% of every stage.** The auto-clear rocket triggers at 95%. Stop at 95%, grab the rocket, advance.

2. **Opening Fuel Supply Chests early.** Open Fuel disappears at event end. Only open chests when you have an opportunity to burn the Fuel inside.

3. **Using Rocket V2 on early stages.** V2 Rockets are rare and carry over between events. Save them for Stage 17+ pushes.

4. **Ignoring Survivors.** Each Survivor adds **+10% snow-clearing radius**. Rescuing all 5 in a stage dramatically reduces Fuel cost.

5. **Pure Snowbuster + Gloves upgrade strategy.** Skipping Coat means slower Fuel regen across the entire event. For Stage 16+ targets, Coat-first is significantly better.

6. **Not stacking Super Snowbuster + Infini-Fuel.** These are designed to be used together. Triggering them separately wastes 50% of their value.

7. **Pushing to Stage 17-18 just because you can.** Reward-per-Fuel ratio is bad. Either stop at 16 or save up Rocket V2 to push to 19.

8. **Burning Coal upgrades on Fire Pit early.** The Fire Pit auto-clears its own area. Coal that doesn't get spent on Fire Pit upgrades **converts to Fuel at end of stage**. Save Coal until you actually need a Fire Pit upgrade.

9. **Forgetting that gear resets every event.** Each Snowbusters event is a fresh start for gear. Don't carry "I'm already maxed" assumptions from last event into this one.

10. **Walking in straight lines.** Forward speed drops while clearing snow but turning speed doesn't. Sweep in arcs to cover more ground per Fuel unit.
