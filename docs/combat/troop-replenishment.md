# Troop Replenishment

::: info Quick take
You can send additional troops to reinforce an existing garrison without attaching a hero. The troops merge into the garrison and benefit from the existing leader's hero skills. This lets you sustain a defense during prolonged combat without pulling your hero out of the fight.
:::

## What This Is

During sustained combat events, your garrison takes losses. Troops get wounded, your troop count drops, and your defensive position weakens. Normally, you'd have to recall your hero, heal, and re-deploy — which takes your hero out of combat entirely.

Instead, you can send a **heroless troop march** to reinforce the garrison. The troops arrive, merge into the existing force, and benefit from the garrison leader's hero skills and stat bonuses.

## How To Do It

### Step 1 — Open the march screen

Tap the building or city where your garrison is deployed.

### Step 2 — Select troops but do NOT assign a hero

Choose the troops you want to send (match troop type to your garrison's needs — usually Infantry). Leave the hero slot empty.

### Step 3 — Send the march

The troops travel to the target and merge into the existing garrison. No hero required.

## How It Works Mechanically

When troops arrive at a garrisoned position, the game's replenishment logic follows a specific order:

- **Highest troop level first** — your best troops fill first
- **Then by troop type** — if troops share the same level, Infantry fills before Lancer, Lancer before Marksman

The reinforcing troops adopt the garrison leader's hero buffs. They don't need their own hero to benefit from the leader's Expedition skills.

## When To Use It

- **Sustaining a garrison during a siege** — your troops take hits from repeated rallies. Send fresh Infantry to keep the defense alive without removing your hero.
- **Refilling after taking a rally** — a big enemy rally just hit your garrison. You've lost half your Infantry. Send a heroless Infantry march to restore your frontline.
- **Supporting an ally's defense** — they're under attack and need more troops. Send your reserves without committing a hero you need elsewhere.

## When NOT To Use It

- **Don't send heroless troops on offense.** Offensive rallies need the leader's hero — heroless reinforcement only makes sense for garrison/defense.
- **Don't send heroless troops to an empty position.** With no hero present, the troops have zero hero buffs. Always ensure a hero is already garrisoned before sending reinforcements.
- **Don't send low-tier troops.** Sending T5 into a T10 garrison wastes infirmary space when they get wounded. Send your highest available tier.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not checking hospital capacity first.** If your Infirmary is full, wounded troops die instead of being hospitalized. Check capacity before committing more troops to a fight.

2. **Sending the wrong troop type.** If your garrison needs Infantry (to absorb frontline damage), sending Marksman doesn't help. Match the reinforcement type to what the garrison needs.

3. **Forgetting this option exists.** Most players default to recalling their hero and re-deploying from scratch. Heroless reinforcement is faster and keeps your hero in the fight.
