# Rally Mechanics

::: info Quick take
Rallies combine multiple players' troops under one leader. The leader contributes all their hero skills; joiners contribute only their first hero's first Expedition skill. Understanding this distinction is the single most important PvP concept in the game.
:::

## What a Rally Is

A rally is a coordinated multi-player attack. One player creates the rally (the **leader**), and other players reinforce it (the **joiners**). The leader's hero uses ALL their Expedition skills, and their gear, stats, and hero level determine the rally's base power. Joiners fill the rally with additional troops.

Rally capacity is determined by the leader's Embassy level plus deployment buffs (pets, research, VIP, etc.).

## The Leader vs. Joiner Distinction

This is the mechanic that most players misunderstand, and it changes everything about how you approach rallies.

**Rally Leader:**
- Uses ALL of their hero's Expedition skills
- Their hero gear, level, stars, and widget all matter
- Their pet buffs and gem buffs apply to the entire rally
- Embassy level determines rally capacity

**Rally Joiner:**
- Only their **first hero's first Expedition skill** contributes to the rally
- Their hero gear is **completely irrelevant** — it does nothing
- Their hero level and stars only matter for Expedition skill unlock gates
- They contribute troops (more HP = more damage absorption = longer rally lifespan)

## The Top-4 Skill Mechanic

The rally doesn't use every joiner's first Expedition skill. It selects the **top 4 highest-level** first Expedition skills from all joiners. Later joiners with higher-level skills automatically replace weaker ones from earlier joiners.

This means:
- If 10 players join but only 4 have Level 4+ first Expedition skills, only those 4 skills count
- A Level 5 skill from a late joiner replaces a Level 3 skill from an earlier joiner
- The rally is constantly optimizing for the best available skills

::: warning Level 5 lock-in risk
If a player joins with the wrong hero and that hero's first Expedition skill is Level 5, it permanently locks into the rally's skill pool. Only another Level 5 skill can replace it, or the player must be kicked from the rally. One wrong pick wastes a buff slot for everyone.
:::

## Your Best Leader ≠ Your Best Joiner

This is the insight that separates competitive players from casual ones.

Heroes like **Flint** and **Natalia** are excellent rally leaders — their full Expedition skill suites are powerful. But as joiners, they're poor choices. Flint's first Expedition skill (Pyromaniac) is a chance-based fire DoT effect, not a flat buff. His best skill (Burning Resolve, +25% Attack) is his second Expedition skill — which only matters for leading, not joining.

The best joiners are heroes whose **first** Expedition skill provides a strong flat buff. See [Rally Joining](/combat/rally-joining) for the complete tier list.

## When to Rally vs. Solo

Rallies consume more event resources than solo attacks. In Mercenary Prestige, a rally costs 25 stamina vs. 10 for a solo (and failed solos refund their stamina). Rally when you need combined force; solo when you're confident in the outcome.

General rule: rally for PvP targets (other players, contested buildings, Castle Battle), solo for PvE content you can handle alone (beasts, lower-tier Mercenary waves).

## Garrison Mechanics

Garrison battles work similarly to rallies but for defense. The player garrisoning with the highest stat bonuses becomes the source of defense buffs. Additionally, 4 primary hero skills from other garrison members activate for the battle — the same top-4 selection mechanic as rallies.

Garrison troops are replenished by highest level first, then by troop type. If troops share the same level, replenishment follows this order: Infantry, Lancer, Marksman.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Assuming your strongest hero = best joiner.** It's the first Expedition skill that matters, not overall hero power. A well-built Jessie (Stand at Arms: +25% damage dealt) is infinitely more valuable as a joiner than a maxed Flint.

2. **Not checking which hero is in slot 1.** The game uses your slot-1 hero by default. If your slot-1 hero is your gathering hero instead of your rally joiner, you've wasted a contribution.

3. **Ignoring the "send troops only" option.** If you don't have a hero with a good first Expedition skill, sending troops with no hero is better than sending the wrong hero. An empty slot is neutral; a wrong hero is actively harmful.

4. **Gearing up your joiner hero.** Joiner gear does nothing. Don't waste enhancement materials on a hero you only use for joining rallies.
