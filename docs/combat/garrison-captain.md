# Garrison Captain

::: info Quick take
The joiner question is easy because a joiner only contributes one thing — their first hero's first Expedition skill (Patrick at this generation). The captain question is genuinely harder because the captain contributes **three** hero skills to the entire garrison, so "who" is a trio question, not a single name. The captain trio at Gen 2/3 is **Sergey + Patrick + (Logan or Flint)**, infantry-led, running 60/40/0 troop ratio.
:::

## Why The Captain Seat Is Murkier Than The Joiner Seat

When a Bear Trap rally or an attacking rally happens, every joiner contributes exactly one thing: the first Expedition skill of their lead hero. The rally selects the top-4 highest-level first-skills across all joiners. So as a joiner, the answer is always "lead the hero whose first skill matches what's needed." For offensive: Jessie. For defensive: Patrick.

A garrison captain, by contrast, contributes **all three of their lead hero's Expedition skills**, plus their gear, Chief Gear, hero level/stars, and pet/gem buffs flow into the entire garrison via the Bonus Source mechanic (see [Battle Reports](/combat/battle-reports#the-bonus-source-mechanic)).

So the question "who should the garrison captain run as their 3 heroes?" is actually a **roster-optimization** problem with three slots, not a single-name lookup. That's why the captain answer feels less settled — there isn't one "best hero," there's a best trio.

## What Defensive Skills Actually Matter

The captain's three heroes contribute *all* their Expedition skills to the entire garrison. So what you want is three heroes whose **combined Expedition skill suites** stack the right defensive multipliers. The defensive math wants:

- **Troop Health bonuses** (more pool to absorb damage)
- **Troop Defense bonuses** (less damage per hit taken)
- **Damage Reduction bonuses** (flat damage reduction)
- **Enemy debuffs** (reduce their Attack/damage)

You do **not** want offensive skills like Lethality buffs or Attack proc skills, because those skills are wasted on a hold. Your job is to outlast the rally, not to kill it. Time is your weapon — every minute the garrison survives is another minute the enemy spent troops and buffs they don't get back.

## The Captain Trio By Generation

### Gen 1/2 Server (No Logan yet)

**Trio: Sergey + Patrick + Flint (or Bahiti)**

- **Sergey** — Expedition skill 1 is Defenders' Edge (-20% damage taken for all troops). Anchor. Lead with him.
- **Patrick** — Expedition skill 1 is Super Nutrients (+25% troop Health). His widget at max level adds another +15% Health to garrisoned units, which stacks with the skill.
- **Flint** — placeholder body if you're short on dedicated defensive heroes. His Burning Resolve is a +25% Attack flat buff which is wasted on defense, but he's a strong frame to absorb damage. Slot him third.
- **Bahiti** as alternative — defensive Expedition skills include damage reduction (up to 20%) and a chance-based damage boost. Better defensive role than Flint, weaker frame.

Lead **Sergey** (slot 1). His Defenders' Edge is the strongest defensive first-Expedition skill available to most rosters.

### Gen 3 Server (Logan unlocked)

**Trio: Logan + Sergey + Patrick**

- **Logan** — Expedition skills are Lion Intimidation (-20% damage taken for all troops, *flat*, no proc), Leader Inspiration (+25% Health for all troops), and Lion Strike (-20% enemy troop Attack). All three skills are defensive, all flat (no proc gambling), all account-wide.
- **Sergey** — Defenders' Edge remains the strongest Gen 1 defensive skill. He still leads Bonus Source math if his power is highest, but Logan typically becomes the Bonus Source if he's developed.
- **Patrick** — Super Nutrients still adds Health.

Lead **Logan** if his combat power exceeds Sergey's (likely once he's at 3★+ with Mythic Infantry gear). Logan's widget at Lv2 adds +15% Defender Troop Defense — *specifically* a garrison bonus, exactly the role we're talking about.

::: tip Logan's widget Lv2 is the garrison move
Logan's "Strong Protection" skill (unlocked at exclusive gear Lv2) adds +15% Defender Troop Defense — only active when garrisoning. If you have Logan and you anticipate doing garrison captain duty in SvS or facility defense, prioritize his widget to Lv2 before anything else on him.
:::

### Castle Defense / City Barricade

Your city is conceptually the same as a garrison — your barricade is a garrison with you as captain. The hero selection is identical: defensive trio, infantry-led, Sergey + Patrick + (Logan or Flint).

This is also where **Philly's widget** specifically pays off — Philly's exclusive gear at max adds +15% Health to garrisoned units, GARRISON ONLY. If you have a developed Philly, she's worth slotting into a city defense lineup specifically.

## What NOT To Use The Garrison Captain Seat For

**Do not** run your attack trio (Flint / Molly / Alonzo at Gen 2; Flint / Mia / Greg at Gen 3) as garrison captain. That trio is tuned for dealing damage in rallies — offensive Lethality, attack procs, damage amplification. On a hold, those skills are wasted because the goal isn't to kill, it's to outlast.

Other heroes to keep OUT of the captain seat:

- **Gina** — Exploration-focused, her Expedition kit is weak for defense
- **Jasser** — strong as an offensive joiner, weaker as a defensive captain
- **Mia / Molly** — Lancer damage dealers, not defenders
- **Greg / Alonzo** — Marksman damage dealers, not defenders
- **Jessie** — best as an offensive joiner (Stand at Arms = +25% all damage)

The pattern: the heroes that make great rally leaders or great offensive joiners are usually *not* good garrison captains. Different skills are wanted.

## Joiner Heroes For Garrisons

While the captain question is the focus of this page, the joiner question is worth restating because the answer is so clean: **Patrick.** Every garrison joiner should lead Patrick if they have him. Super Nutrients's +25% Health is the strongest universal defensive first-Expedition skill in the game at this generation.

If you don't have Patrick: **Sergey** with Defenders' Edge (-20% damage taken) is the next-best joiner first-skill. Two Sergeys (or one Patrick + one Sergey) in a garrison stack both buffs since they're different skills.

Beyond Patrick and Sergey, garrison joiners are essentially "send troops as bulk." The first Expedition skills of most other heroes don't pay off defensively.

::: warning Don't send the wrong hero as a Level 5 garrison joiner
If a joiner sends a Level 5 first Expedition skill that doesn't belong (e.g., Flint's Pyromaniac — a chance-based fire DoT), it locks into the garrison's top-4 skill pool and crowds out a real defensive buff. Same mechanic as offensive rallies. If you don't have Patrick or Sergey, send no hero (send troops only) rather than the wrong hero.
:::

## Defense Troop Ratios

Defense ratios differ from offensive ratios for a clear reason: in a hold, **Marksmen die before they fire**. The fight resolves at the Infantry/Lancer interaction, so committing troops to Marksman slots is committing troops that contribute zero.

### The Three Standard Defense Ratios

| Ratio | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **60/40/0** | Standard contested facility, balanced threat | Workhorse defensive composition; tanks well, lancers add counter-Marksman work |
| **70/30/0** | Sustained brutal pressure, slow defenders, hospital pressure | Maximizes time-to-die; reduces lancer counter-damage |
| **60/20/20** | Light defensive — punish unbalanced attacks | Some Marksman counter-damage if attackers bring weak Infantry; less survivable overall |

For "highly contested" defense (multiple rallies expected, must hold for a window), **60/40/0** is the default. Move to 70/30/0 only if the facility/turret is being grinded down faster than you can heal.

### Why 0% Marksman On Defense

Marksmen attack last in combat order (Infantry → Lancers → Marksmen). In a contested defense, the Infantry/Lancer exchange typically decides the fight before Marksmen fire. Marksmen committed to defense are essentially body weight that doesn't contribute damage and is the most fragile to backline penetration if your Infantry breaks.

The exception is **60/20/20** when you specifically want some Marksman counter-damage to punish an attacker that brought too few Lancers or unbalanced their composition. This is a niche pick — most garrisons run 60/40/0.

### What The Joiners Run

**Mirror the captain ratio.** If the captain runs 60/40/0, joiners should run 60/40/0. The garrison fights as a single force — having one joiner run 90/10/0 because they're "trying to help with damage" actually weakens the defensive line.

Two things to communicate to joiners:

1. **Gear is irrelevant** — only their lead hero's first Expedition skill counts (top-4 mechanic). Don't worry about whether their Patrick is +60 or +75 — only the skill level matters.
2. **Their job is bulk plus a defensive first-skill** — Patrick or Sergey lead, 60/40/0 troops. That's it.

## Pre-Lock Preparation

Garrisons lock when the rally is incoming. Pre-lock window prep:

**Pet skills:**
- **Mammoth** (+10% troop Defense) — activate before garrison locks
- **Frost Gorilla** (+10% troop Health) — activate before garrison locks

**Gem buffs:**
- **Damage Reduction Up III** — defensive buff, 12-hour duration recommended for SvS
- **Troop Health Up III** — defensive buff
- **Defense Up III** — defensive buff

**Important:** for SvS Castle Battle and the SvS 24-hour vulnerability windows, use **12-hour gem buffs**, not the 2-hour ones. The 12-hour duration covers the full event window; a 2-hour buff will expire mid-fight and reset you to your unbuffed state.

**Heroes set:**
- Sergey (or Logan) in slot 1
- Patrick in slot 2
- Defensive third (Logan or Flint) in slot 3
- Confirm widget loadout — Logan widget Lv2 if you have it, Patrick widget Lv10 if you have it

**Troops set:**
- 60/40/0 (or 70/30/0 if pressure is brutal)
- Confirm troop count matches your full garrison capacity

**Hospital empty:**
- Heal any wounded troops before the rally lands. A full hospital can't absorb new casualties — new losses become permanent.

## The 24-Hour Window Scenario

A common scenario for contested defenses is the SvS vulnerability window — a 24-hour period when your alliance's facility or castle is open to attacks.

**For the captain:**
- Stay logged in or set a reliable second device awake during the window
- Pop 12-hour gem buffs to cover most of the window with a single activation
- Re-pop pet skills every 2 hours (they have ~20-hour cooldown — you get two activations per window)
- Heal between waves; never start a fight with hospital full

**For joiners:**
- Coordinate Patrick-lead, defensive ratio, 60/40/0
- Bodies matter — fill the garrison to capacity
- Don't try to attack other targets while your garrison is the focus of incoming rallies (your troops can't be in two places)

## City Defense — Same Rules Apply

The captain question maps directly to **city defense** — your barricade is conceptually a one-person garrison. Same hero selection, same ratio, same pet/gem buffs. The only difference is alliance reinforcements can't physically join your city *after* combat starts, so set the garrison reinforcement list well before any threat.

If you have a strong alliance member who you trust to defend you, ask them to reinforce your city pre-emptively. Their stats become your Bonus Source if their combat power exceeds yours — and you get to fight at their stats. This is how "small-power players hide behind whales" in coordinated alliances.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Running the attack trio (Flint/Mia/Greg or Flint/Molly/Alonzo) as captain.** Offensive skills wasted on defense. Use defensive specialists.
2. **Letting joiners pick their own hero.** If a joiner sends Flint as their hero with a Lv5 first Expedition skill (Pyromaniac), it locks into the garrison's top-4 skill pool. Coordinate Patrick (or "send no hero") for joiners.
3. **Using 2-hour gem buffs for SvS 24-hour windows.** They expire and you fight unbuffed for most of the window. Use 12-hour buffs.
4. **Skipping the hospital-empty check.** A full hospital means new casualties die permanently. Heal between fights.
5. **Sending Marksmen on defense.** Marksmen fire last in combat order. In contested defense, the fight ends before they fire. 60/40/0 means no Marksmen.
6. **Picking the captain by power level instead of role.** "I have the highest power" is the right reason to take the *flag* (Garrison Leader slot), not necessarily the right reason to run your attack hero loadout. Power gives you the Bonus Source, but you should still use defensive heroes.
7. **Running Philly's widget on a Lancer attack rally.** Philly's widget is GARRISON ONLY. It does nothing on an offensive rally.
