# Garrison Swap

::: info Quick take
You can swap your troops from attack to defense mid-march by sending a garrison march to the target, then recalling your attack march just before the garrison arrives. This repositions your troops as defenders instead of attackers — critical when the tactical situation changes during live events.
:::

## What This Is

During live combat events (SvS, Foundry Battle, Sunfire Castle), the situation can change fast. You sent an attack march, but now you need those troops defending instead. You can't just "switch" a march's stance — but you can exploit march timing to achieve the same result.

The technique uses a simple sequence: send the defensive march, then recall the offensive march at the right moment.

## How To Do It

### Step 1 — Send a garrison march

While your attack march is still in transit (or fighting), send a new garrison march to the building or city you want to defend. Use your garrison hero and defensive troop ratio (60/20/20 or 70/30/0).

### Step 2 — Watch the garrison march timer

Your garrison march has a travel timer. Watch it count down.

### Step 3 — Recall your attack march

When your garrison march is roughly **10 seconds from arriving**, recall your attack march. The exact window depends on distances involved — the key is that your attack troops must begin returning **after** the garrison arrives and deploys.

### Step 4 — Garrison deploys, attack returns

Your garrison march arrives and your troops establish defensive position. Your recalled attack troops return home (or to wherever they came from). Your position at the target is now defensive instead of offensive.

## When To Use It

- **Mid-SvS** when the tactical situation shifts (you were attacking a building but now need to hold it against an incoming rally)
- **Foundry Battle** when you need to pivot from offense to defense after capturing a building
- **Sunfire Castle** when timing a garrison rotation during a contested hold

## When NOT To Use It

- If the target is about to fall anyway — better to let the attack finish
- If you don't have a garrison hero ready — a garrison without defensive hero skills is weak
- If your attack is almost at the target — the recall won't save time, just commit

## Common Mistakes

1. **Recalling the attack march too early.** If your attack troops arrive home before the garrison deploys, you've accomplished nothing except wasting march time. The recall needs to happen late enough that the garrison beats the returning troops.

2. **Not switching troop ratios.** Your attack march was probably 50/20/30. Your garrison should be 60/20/20 or 70/30/0. Sending a garrison with an offensive ratio defeats the purpose.

3. **Not assigning a garrison hero.** A garrison without a defensive hero (Patrick, Sergey) is significantly weaker. Make sure your garrison march has the right hero.

4. **Trying this with too-distant targets.** The technique works best at short to medium distances where you can reasonably time the 10-second window. At long distances, the timing becomes impractical — better to send a separate garrison and let the attack finish naturally.

## Worked Example

You're in Foundry Battle. You sent an attack march to Prototype Site 1 with Flint (50/20/30). Your alliance just captured it, and now an enemy rally is incoming. You need garrison defense, not an attack march sitting on the building.

1. Send a garrison march with Sergey + 60/20/20 troops to Prototype Site 1
2. Watch the garrison timer — your travel time is about 45 seconds
3. At ~35 seconds elapsed (10 seconds remaining), recall your Flint attack march
4. Sergey's garrison arrives and deploys defensively
5. Flint's troops return home, available for the next rally

Now Prototype Site 1 has a proper defensive garrison with Sergey's damage reduction, not an attack march with an offensive hero and ratio.
